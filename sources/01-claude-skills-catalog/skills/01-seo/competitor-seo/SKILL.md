# Competitor SEO Analysis

SEO competitive intelligence. This skill identifies where competitors rank that you don't, what content they've built that you haven't, what SERP features they own, and where their structural advantages can be matched or beaten.

## When to Use This Skill

- Finding keyword gaps where competitors rank and you don't
- Identifying content topics competitors cover that you haven't
- Understanding why a competitor outranks you for a target keyword
- Discovering SERP features (snippets, PAA) competitors own
- Analyzing content formats and structures that dominate your niche
- Prioritizing content investments based on what's actually working for competitors
- Finding weaknesses in competitor content you can exploit

## Competitor Selection

Before analysis, identify the right competitors:

**Types of SEO competitors:**

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Direct business competitor | Competes for same customers | Your industry rival |
| SERP competitor | Ranks for same keywords, different business | Industry blog, review site |
| Authority site | Dominates your topics broadly | Forbes, Healthline, NerdWallet |

**Find your real SERP competitors:**
1. Search your 5 most important target keywords
2. Note which domains appear in top 5 results most frequently
3. These are your actual SERP competitors — not necessarily your business competitors
4. A comparison site that ranks for every query in your space is a more important SEO competitor than a direct business rival with a weak site

## Keyword Gap Analysis

Identifying keywords where competitors rank and you don't.

### Gap Categories

| Gap Type | Description | Priority |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| High-volume missed keywords | Competitor ranks top 10, you don't rank at all | High |
| SERP feature gaps | Competitor owns featured snippet or PAA you could win | High |
| Long-tail clusters | Groups of related keywords competitor dominates | Medium |
| Commercial intent gaps | Transactional/commercial keywords competitor captures | High |
| Informational gaps | Informational queries feeding their funnel | Medium |

### Manual Keyword Gap Research

Without premium tools, you can still identify gaps:

**Method 1: SERP scanning**
1. List your 20 most important target keywords
2. Search each in Google
3. Note which competitors appear in top 5
4. Look for patterns: which topics do they rank for that you don't even have content about?

**Method 2: Competitor content audit**
1. Browse competitor's blog/resources section
2. Note every topic they've published content on
3. Check which of those topics you've covered
4. Gaps = their topics, your missing content

**Method 3: People Also Ask mining**
1. Search your target keyword
2. Open every PAA question
3. Note which competitors answer those questions
4. These are content gaps with confirmed search intent

**Method 4: site: search**
```
site:competitor.com [topic keyword]
```
Shows you every page they have on a topic. Compare to what you have.

### Keyword Gap Prioritization

Score each gap keyword on:

| Factor | High (3) | Medium (2) | Low (1) |
|--------|---------|-----------|--------|
| Search volume | 1,000+/mo | 100-999/mo | <100/mo |
| Commercial intent | Transactional/commercial | Mixed | Informational |
| Ranking difficulty | Weak competitor content | Moderate content | Strong content |
| Your ability to rank | Strong domain/topical authority | Moderate | Weak |
| Revenue potential | Directly tied to service | Related | Tangential |

**Prioritize gaps where you score 12-15.** Ignore gaps where you score under 8 — they're not worth the content investment given your competitive position.

## SERP Feature Analysis

Owning SERP features means more visibility without needing the #1 ranking.

### SERP Feature Types

| Feature | How to Win It | Competitor Advantage |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| Featured Snippet | Direct answer in content, clear H2 question, proper formatting | They own the "position 0" click |
| People Also Ask | Content that answers related questions directly | They capture PAA clicks across the topic |
| Image Pack | High-quality, relevant images with good alt text | They get image search traffic |
| Video Carousel | YouTube video ranking for query | They get video visibility |
| Local Pack | GBP optimization, local signals | They capture local intent queries |
| Knowledge Panel | Entity establishment, Wikipedia, authoritative sources | They own the brand query space |
| Sitelinks | Strong brand + site structure | Their homepage takes more real estate |

### SERP Feature Gap Research

For your 20 most important keywords:
1. Search each keyword
2. Note which SERP features appear
3. Note if a competitor owns them
4. Assess whether you can compete for that feature

**Featured Snippet opportunity assessment:**
- Does a snippet exist for this query? (If no: opportunity to create one)
- Who owns the current snippet?
- How strong is their content? (Weak = you can displace it)
- Can you format your content to directly answer the query in snippet format?

**Snippet formats that work:**
- Paragraph snippets: 40-60 word direct answer
- List snippets: Numbered or bulleted list of steps/items
- Table snippets: Comparison or data in table format
- Video snippets: YouTube video answering the query

## Competitive Content Quality Assessment

Understanding *why* competitor content ranks — not just *that* it ranks.

### Quality Signals to Analyze

For each competitor page that outranks you:

| Signal | What to Look For |
|--------|----------------|
| Word count | Significantly longer? Or same length, higher quality? |
| Content format | Different format (list vs. guide vs. tool vs. calculator)? |
| Original data | Do they have proprietary data, studies, or research? |
| Visual assets | More images, videos, infographics? Custom graphics? |
| Author authority | Named author with visible credentials? |
| Update frequency | Content regularly updated with fresh date? |
| User signals | High-engagement elements (calculators, tools, quizzes)? |
| Internal linking | Better connected to related content on their site? |
| Backlinks | Significantly more authoritative external links? |

### The "Why They Win" Framework

For any competitor page outranking you, identify which of these explains it:

1. **Better content** — More complete, more specific, more useful
2. **Better authority** — More backlinks, more trusted domain
3. **Better format match** — Their format fits what searchers want, yours doesn't
4. **Better topical authority** — They have more content on the topic, establishing deeper expertise
5. **Better technical signals** — Faster, better structured, better schema
6. **Better freshness** — Recently updated vs. your stale content

Each cause has a different fix. "Better content" means rewrite. "Better authority" means build links. "Better format" means change your page structure.

## Backlink Profile Comparison

Backlinks remain a major ranking factor. Competitive gaps here matter.

### What to Compare

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-----------------|
| Total referring domains | Their overall link authority vs. yours |
| Link quality distribution | High-authority links vs. low-quality spam |
| Anchor text patterns | What people say about them when linking |
| Content that earns links | Which of their pages earn the most links |
| Link velocity | How fast they're acquiring new links |

### Finding Competitor Link Opportunities

**Technique 1: Broken link building**
1. Find pages that link to competitor content
2. Find pages on their site that have gone 404
3. Create equivalent content, outreach to sites pointing to the dead page

**Technique 2: Link intersect**
1. Find sites that link to multiple competitors but not you
2. These sites are pre-qualified — they link to your space
3. Outreach with your best equivalent content

**Technique 3: Link to their data, not their conclusions**
1. Find competitor content that got links because of original data
2. Conduct your own research on the same topic
3. Newer data often earns links from the same sites that linked to older research

## Competitive Positioning Strategy

After analysis, choose where to compete and where not to.

### Position Options

| Position | When to Use |
|----------|------------|
| **Direct competition** — Target same keywords | You have comparable authority and can produce better content |
| **Flanking** — Target adjacent keywords they're weak on | They dominate core terms but leave related terms undefended |
| **Long-tail focus** — Own specific, lower-volume queries they ignore | They pursue volume; you pursue specificity and conversion |
| **Format differentiation** — Win by format, not keyword | They rank with articles; you win with tools, calculators, videos |
| **Freshness play** — Maintain newer content on fast-moving topics | Their content is stale; you keep content current |

### Where NOT to Compete

Don't fight battles you can't win:

- Keywords where authority sites (Forbes, Healthline, NerdWallet) dominate and you have no structural path to compete
- Topics where competitors have 10x your domain authority and 5x your content depth
- Queries where the SERP is dominated by brand navigational intent
- Keywords where paid ads take the entire visible screen (organic value is minimal)

**Spend resources where you can actually win.** A page that ranks #1 for a niche long-tail term drives more value than a page stuck at #40 for a high-volume head term.

## Red Flags

Common mistakes in competitive SEO analysis:

- [ ] Targeting identical keywords as competitors with 10x your domain authority
- [ ] Ignoring long-tail opportunities where you can actually win
- [ ] Copying competitor content strategy without understanding their structural advantages
- [ ] No awareness of which SERP features competitors own
- [ ] Competing on volume instead of depth and specificity
- [ ] Analyzing only direct business competitors, not actual SERP competitors
- [ ] Treating all keyword gaps as equal priority regardless of intent or difficulty
- [ ] Ignoring the "why they win" analysis — just trying to out-word-count them
- [ ] Building content without checking if the SERP format matches your approach
- [ ] Not tracking whether you're closing the gaps after implementing recommendations
