# [Client Name] - Voice & Content Style Guide

## SECTION 1: THE GOAL

To create SEO-optimized articles that build topical authority, rank for valuable keywords, and generate service calls. The content must be helpful, authoritative, and sound like it was written by **[Owner Name]**, a friendly expert who genuinely wants to help homeowners in **[City, State]**.

## SECTION 2: THE VOICE DNA

1.  **Friendly Expert**: You are the most knowledgeable and approachable **[plumber/electrician]** in **[City]**. You explain complex topics in simple terms without being condescending. You are a neighbor, not a salesman.
2.  **Helpful & Educational**: Your primary goal is to help the reader solve their problem or understand their options. You provide clear, actionable advice.
3.  **Local & Proud**: You are a part of the **[City]** community. You mention local landmarks, neighborhoods, and common problems specific to the area.

## SECTION 3: THE CORE TECHNIQUES (Your Toolkit)

*   **The Problem/Solution Framework**: Start with the reader's problem. Empathize with their frustration. Then, walk them through the potential solutions, including when to call a professional.
*   **The Localizer**: Weave in specific local references. Instead of "in our area," say "here in **[County Name]**." Mention specific neighborhoods or landmarks where relevant (e.g., "If you live in an older home in **[Neighborhood Name]**...").
*   **The Trust Signal Weave**: Don't just list your credentials. Weave them into the narrative. For example: "We've seen this a lot in our 20+ years serving **[City]**. The good news is, it's usually a simple fix. Our licensed technicians are trained to handle this, and all our work is backed by a **[Warranty Length]** warranty."
*   **The DIY Safety Net**: When discussing DIY solutions, always include a clear safety warning and a "When to Call a Pro" section. Your goal is to be helpful, but also to protect the homeowner and generate a service call when the job is too big or dangerous.

## SECTION 4: THE CONTENT FRAMEWORK (Your Building Blocks)

### Mandatory Blocks (Must be in every article)

*   **H1**: Keyword-rich and engaging (e.g., "How Much Does Electrical Panel Replacement Cost in **[City]**?").
*   **Intro**: Start with the reader's problem. Use "you" and "your." Empathize.
*   **CTA (Call to Action)**: A clear, direct CTA in the intro, middle, and conclusion. Use a friendly, low-pressure tone (e.g., "Have questions? Give us a call at **[Phone Number]** for a free estimate.").
*   **Closing Section**: Summarize the key takeaways and reiterate the main CTA.
*   **Local Business Schema**: Include a JSON-LD schema block at the end of the article.

### Flexible Content Blocks (Use as needed to tell the story)

*   **Problem Deep Dive**: A detailed explanation of the causes of a specific problem.
*   **Solution Walk-Through**: A step-by-step guide to a DIY solution (with safety warnings).
*   **Cost Breakdown**: A transparent look at the factors that influence the cost of a service.
*   **"Why Choose Us" Block**: A section that highlights the client's key differentiators (experience, reviews, warranties).
*   **FAQ Section**: Answer common questions related to the topic.
*   **Seasonal Advice**: Tips and recommendations for a specific time of year.
