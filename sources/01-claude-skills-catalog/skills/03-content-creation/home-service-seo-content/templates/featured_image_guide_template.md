# Featured Image Generation Guide (Home Service)

## THE GOAL

To create a unique, high-quality featured image for each article that is visually compelling, relevant to the service, and builds trust. The image should look professional and authentic, not like a generic stock photo.

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE: AUTHENTICITY & HELPFULNESS

The image should show the service being performed correctly and safely, or it should visually explain a concept in a helpful way. Avoid cheesy stock photos of smiling technicians with fake customers. The goal is to build trust and authority.

## THE PROCESS

### Step 1: Read the Article & Identify the Core Visual

What is the single most important visual element of the article? Is it a specific part, a tool, a process, or a problem?

- **For a service page:** The visual should show the service being performed by a clean, professional technician.
- **For a troubleshooting guide:** The visual should clearly show the part or problem being discussed.
- **For a cost guide:** An infographic or a clean photo of a relevant part (e.g., a new electrical panel).
- **For a DIY guide:** A diagram or a series of photos showing the key steps (with safety gear).

### Step 2: Choose a Visual Approach

**A. The Clean Action Shot:**
- **When to use:** For service pages or articles about a specific job.
- **What it is:** A professional photograph of a technician performing the service. The focus is on the hands and the work area. The technician is in a clean uniform. The lighting is bright and clear.
- **Example:** A close-up shot of an electrician's hands wiring an electrical panel. The background is slightly out of focus.

**B. The Helpful Diagram/Infographic:**
- **When to use:** For educational articles, cost guides, or DIY guides.
- **What it is:** A clean, simple illustration or diagram that explains a concept. Use the client's brand colors.
- **Example:** An infographic showing the factors that influence the cost of a new HVAC system.

**C. The "Problem" Shot:**
- **When to use:** For articles about a specific problem (e.g., a leaking pipe, a flickering light).
- **What it is:** A clear, well-lit photo of the problem itself. This helps the reader immediately identify with the issue.
- **Example:** A photo of water damage on a ceiling below a bathroom.

### Step 3: Write a Detailed, Specific Prompt

1.  **Visual Approach:** Start with the chosen approach (e.g., "professional close-up photograph," "clean and simple infographic," "well-lit photo").
2.  **Subject:** The main subject, with specific details (e.g., "an electrician's hands wearing clean gloves," "a modern, high-efficiency water heater").
3.  **Setting:** The environment (e.g., "in a clean, well-lit basement," "against a plain white background").
4.  **Lighting:** Bright, clean, and professional.
5.  **Composition:** Focus on the key details. Use a shallow depth of field to blur the background if needed.
6.  **Mood:** Helpful, professional, and trustworthy.
7.  **Exclusions:** "no faces," "no cheesy smiles," "no fake customers," "no brand names on tools."

### Step 4: Run the 5-Point Quality Check

1.  **Authenticity:** Does this look like a real photo from a real job, or a cheesy stock photo? If it looks fake, regenerate.
2.  **Relevance:** Does the image directly relate to the article's main topic?
3.  **Quality:** Is the image high-resolution and well-lit?
4.  **Safety:** If a person is shown, are they using the correct tools and safety equipment?
5.  **Brand Fit:** Does the image feel professional and trustworthy?
