# ProofPilot Proposal Structures

Section-by-section templates derived from real ProofPilot proposals. Follow these patterns exactly.

## Universal Elements (All Proposal Types)

### Cover Section
1. Title prefix in Electric Blue (e.g., "YOUR CUSTOM", "COMPREHENSIVE")
2. Main title in Dark Blue (e.g., "WEBSITE & SEO PROPOSAL", "MARKETING STRATEGY")
3. Italic tagline in Medium Gray (e.g., "Built for [Business Name] | [Month Year]")
4. Client info table:
   - Prepared For: [Business Name]
   - Contact: [Owner Name]
   - Location: [City, State]
   - Prepared By: ProofPilot
   - Date: [Date]

### Value Proposition Box
Dark Blue background, Neon Green headline, White body text.
Headline: "WHY [BUSINESS NAME] NEEDS THIS" or "WHAT YOU GET"
3-5 bullet points summarizing the key deliverables or outcomes.

### Investment Summary (Near the End)
Table format showing:
- Each service line item
- One-time cost (if applicable)
- Monthly cost (if applicable)
- Total row

### Payment Options Section
Always include all three:
1. 12-Month Plan: [monthly amount] x 12 months
2. Pay in Full: [total minus 10%] (Save [dollar amount])
3. 50/50 Split: [half] at kickoff + [half] at launch

### Next Steps
Numbered list, typically 3-5 steps:
1. Review this proposal and let us know your questions
2. Choose your investment option
3. Sign the agreement and submit your first payment
4. We schedule your kickoff call within [X] days
5. Build begins

### CTA Box (Final Element)
Dark Blue background, Neon Green headline.
Headline: "READY TO [ACTION]?" or "LET'S BUILD YOUR [OUTCOME]"
Body lines:
- "Schedule your kickoff call today."
- "ProofPilot | [City, State]"
- "www.proofpilot.com"

## Type 1: Website + SEO Proposal

### Section Flow

**Section 1: The Problem**
- Open with a bold statement about their current situation
- Reference specific audit findings (scores out of 10)
- Example: "Your website scored 3/10 on mobile performance and 2/10 on local SEO visibility."
- Use a table showing their audit scores across key areas

**Section 2: Keywords Your Competitors Own**
- Table showing top competitor's keywords vs. the client's
- Columns: Keyword, Monthly Searches, Competitor Position, Your Position
- Pull from audit data or Ahrefs research
- Body text: "Your top competitor [Name] ranks for [X] keywords. You rank for [Y]."

**Section 3: The Cost of Doing Nothing**
- Calculate lost revenue from missing keywords
- Formula: Missing keywords x avg search volume x estimated CTR x conversion rate x job value
- Present as annual lost revenue
- Optional: Compare cost of ProofPilot to cost of alternatives (TopLine Pro, Scorpion, etc.)

**Section 4: Your New Website**
- Primary recommendation (e.g., Territory Site at $15,000)
- What's included (page count, features, integrations)
- Why this tier is right for them (tied to audit findings)
- Alternative recommendation (e.g., Growth Site at $8,000 if budget is a concern)
- Comparison table: Feature | Growth Site | Territory Site

**Section 5: SEO Strategy (Optional Add-On or Phase 2)**
- Recommended SEO tier
- What's included
- Expected timeline to results (typically 3-6 months for initial movement)
- Position as "Phase 2" that builds on the new website foundation

**Section 6: ROI Projections**
- Two scenarios: Conservative and Realistic
- Table format:
  - Monthly visitors: [number]
  - Conversion rate: [3% / 5%]
  - Monthly leads: [number]
  - Close rate: [30% / 40%]
  - Monthly jobs: [number]
  - Avg job value: [$X]
  - Monthly revenue: [$X]
  - Annual revenue: [$X]
- Bottom line: "Even at conservative estimates, your new website pays for itself in [X] months."

**Section 7: Your Investment**
- Investment summary table
- Payment options
- Next steps

## Type 2: Meta Ads + Funnel Proposal

### Section Flow

**Section 1: The Opportunity**
- Frame Meta advertising as an untapped channel
- Stats about their audience being on Facebook/Instagram
- Example: "Your ideal customers are scrolling Instagram right now looking for [service]. They don't know you exist yet."

**Section 2: Why Meta Ads Work for [Industry]**
- Short explanation of how Meta targeting works for local services
- Interest-based targeting, lookalike audiences, retargeting
- Tie to their specific services

**Section 3: Meta Ads Management**
- Monthly management fee: $1,500/mo
- What's included (strategy, targeting, creative, testing, reporting)
- How the process works month by month

**Section 4: Your Lead Funnels**
- Recommend specific funnels for their top 2-4 services
- Table format:
  - Service | Funnel Type | Estimated CPL | Monthly Leads (at recommended spend)
- Example funnels: "EV Charger Installation Quiz", "Water Heater Replacement Calculator"
- Brief description of how each funnel qualifies leads

**Section 5: Ad Spend Tiers**
- Good/Better/Best format
- Table for each tier:
  - Monthly ad spend
  - Estimated leads
  - Estimated cost per lead
  - Estimated monthly revenue (leads x close rate x avg job value)
  - Estimated ROAS

**Section 6: How the Funnel Works**
- Step-by-step visual flow (numbered 1-4 or 1-5):
  1. Prospect sees ad on Facebook/Instagram
  2. Clicks to quiz/qualification funnel
  3. Answers questions that qualify them
  4. Books appointment or submits info
  5. Your team receives qualified lead with all details
- Use a simple table or numbered list to show this flow

**Section 7: Your Investment**
- Funnel build cost (one-time)
- Monthly ads management
- Recommended starting ad spend
- Total monthly investment
- Payment options
- Next steps

## Type 3: Comprehensive Marketing Strategy

### Section Flow

**Section 1: Where You Are Today**
- Current website assessment (brief, reference audit)
- Current marketing efforts
- Current lead sources
- Pain points from discovery call

**Section 2: Your Top Competitor**
- Pick their strongest local competitor
- Comparison table: Metric | [Competitor] | [Client]
- Metrics: Keywords ranked, monthly traffic, domain authority, Google reviews, service pages, location pages
- Body text explaining the gap and what it means

**Section 3: The Marketing System You Need**
- Overview of all components:
  - Website (foundation)
  - SEO (long-term growth)
  - Paid Ads (immediate leads)
  - Lead Funnels (conversion optimization)
  - Review Strategy (trust building)
- Brief 1-2 sentence description of each and how they work together

**Section 4: The Roadmap**
- 3-Phase approach:
  - Phase 1 (Months 1-2): Website build + Google Ads setup
  - Phase 2 (Months 3-4): SEO launch + content creation begins
  - Phase 3 (Months 5-6): Meta Ads + funnels + scaling
- Table or timeline format showing phases

**Section 5: Website Architecture**
- Recommended site structure
- Service pages list
- Location pages list (if multi-location)
- Combo pages (service + location)
- Total page count
- Which website tier this maps to

**Section 6: Good / Better / Best Options**
- Three investment tiers:
  - Good: Website + Google Ads (foundation)
  - Better: Website + SEO + Google Ads (growth)
  - Best: Website + SEO + Google Ads + Meta Ads + Funnels (domination)
- Table for each tier showing included services and monthly/one-time costs

**Section 7: Detailed Pricing**
- Full breakdown of every line item
- One-time costs vs. monthly costs
- Bundle discounts if applicable
- Maintenance add-on option

**Section 8: ROI Projections**
- Project revenue at 6 months and 12 months
- Conservative and realistic scenarios
- Show the math

**Section 9: Our Guarantee**
- 180-day guarantee language
- What it means for them

**Section 10: Your Investment + Next Steps**
- Recommended tier highlighted
- Payment options
- Next steps
- CTA box

## Type 4: Standalone Lead Funnel Proposal

### Section Flow

**Section 1: Your [Business Type] Today**
- Current client/customer acquisition
- Where leads come from now
- Pain points (inconsistent leads, relying on referrals, no system)

**Section 2: What We Propose**
- Brief overview of the funnel solution
- How it changes their lead flow
- Example: "A dedicated lead generation funnel that captures, qualifies, and books prospects on autopilot."

**Section 3: How the Funnel Works**
- Step-by-step numbered flow:
  1. Prospect finds your ad (or link on your website)
  2. Lands on a custom quiz/assessment page
  3. Answers 5-8 qualifying questions
  4. Qualified leads book directly on your calendar
  5. You receive full lead details before the appointment
- Describe each step in 1-2 sentences

**Section 4: The Math**
- Three scenarios in a table:

| Metric | Conservative | Moderate | Optimistic |
|--------|-------------|----------|------------|
| Monthly ad spend | $X | $X | $X |
| Cost per lead | $X | $X | $X |
| Monthly leads | X | X | X |
| Close rate | X% | X% | X% |
| New clients/mo | X | X | X |
| Avg client value | $X | $X | $X |
| Monthly revenue | $X | $X | $X |

- For recurring revenue businesses (counseling, med spa, etc.), add compounding model:
  - Month 1: X new clients
  - Month 3: X new + Y retained = Z total
  - Month 6: X new + Y retained = Z total
  - Month 12: X new + Y retained = Z total

**Section 5: Your Investment**
- Funnel setup (one-time): $X
- Monthly management: $X/mo (or included with ads)
- Recommended ad spend: $X/mo
- Total monthly after setup: $X/mo
- Payment options for the setup cost

**Section 6: What's Included**
- Bullet list of everything in the management fee:
  - Funnel optimization and A/B testing
  - Ad creative and copy updates
  - Lead quality monitoring
  - Weekly/monthly reporting
  - Strategy adjustments based on data

**Section 7: Next Steps**
- Numbered next steps
- CTA box

## Table Formatting Rules

### Comparison Tables (ProofPilot vs. Competitors)
- 3 columns: Feature/Metric | Competitor | ProofPilot
- Competitor column: neutral formatting
- ProofPilot column: highlight advantages with bold or green text
- Use check marks for features included, "X" for not included

### Scoring/Audit Tables
- 4 columns: Element | Details | Score | Notes
- Header: Dark Blue (odd sections) or Electric Blue (even sections)
- Score row at bottom: Neon Green score text on same colored background
- Individual scores in the Score column: plain text

### Investment Tables
- Columns: Service | One-Time Cost | Monthly Cost
- Header: Dark Blue
- Total row: Bold, slightly larger text
- Use gray alternating rows for readability

### ROI Projection Tables
- Columns: Metric | Conservative | Realistic
- Header: Electric Blue
- Revenue rows: Bold
- Bottom row showing payback period or annual revenue

### Ad Spend Tier Tables
- Columns: Metric | Good | Better | Best
- Header: Dark Blue
- Highlight the recommended tier (bold column header or subtle background)

## Tone Examples From Real Proposals

### Problem Framing (Direct, Data-Driven)
"Right now, your website is invisible. Your top competitor ranks for 847 keywords in Google. You rank for 12. That gap represents hundreds of potential customers finding them instead of you every single month."

### Opportunity Framing (Confident, Forward-Looking)
"Your ideal customers are already on Instagram and Facebook. They need [service] but don't know you exist. Meta Ads put your business in front of them at the exact moment they're thinking about it."

### ROI Framing (Math-Forward)
"At a conservative 3% conversion rate with 500 monthly visitors, your new website generates 15 leads per month. At a 30% close rate, that's 4-5 new jobs. At $3,500 average job value, that's $14,000-$17,500 in monthly revenue from organic traffic alone."

### Investment Framing (Value-Focused)
"TopLine Pro charges $500/mo for a template website you don't own. Scorpion locks you into $3,000/mo contracts with no transparency. Your ProofPilot website is custom-built, fully owned by you, and designed to convert."

### Next Steps (Clear, Action-Oriented)
"1. Review this proposal. Take your time. We want you to feel confident.
2. Choose your investment option.
3. Sign the agreement and submit your initial payment.
4. We schedule your kickoff call within 5 business days.
5. Build begins."
