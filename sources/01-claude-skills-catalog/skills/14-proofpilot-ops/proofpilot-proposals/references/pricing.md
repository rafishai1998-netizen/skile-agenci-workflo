## Paid Advertising (Monthly Recurring)

### Google Ads Management
- Management fee: 15% of monthly ad spend
- Minimum management fee: $500/mo
- Ad spend is separate (paid directly to Google by client)
- Includes: Campaign setup, keyword research, ad copy, bid management, landing page recommendations, monthly reporting

### Local Service Ads (LSA) Management
- Management fee: $500/mo
- Ad spend is separate (paid directly to Google by client)
- Includes: Profile optimization, review management, dispute handling, budget optimization, monthly reporting

### Meta Ads Management (Facebook/Instagram)
- Management fee: $1,500/mo
- Ad spend is separate (paid directly to Meta by client)
- Includes: Campaign strategy, audience targeting, ad creative, A/B testing, funnel integration, monthly reporting
- Often paired with lead funnels (see below)

### Multi-Platform Bundle Discount
- When a client runs ads on 2+ platforms (e.g., Google Ads + Meta Ads, or Google Ads + LSA + Meta Ads)
- Discount: 10% off total management fees
- Example: Google Ads $500/mo + Meta Ads $1,500/mo = $2,000 total, with bundle = $1,800/mo

## Payment Options

Always present these three options in the investment summary:

### Option 1: 12-Month Payment Plan
- Total project cost divided into 12 equal monthly payments
- No interest or fees
- Example: $15,000 website = $1,250/mo for 12 months

### Option 2: Pay in Full
- 10% discount on total project cost
- Example: $15,000 website = $13,500 paid upfront

### Option 3: 50/50 Split
- 50% due at project kickoff
- 50% due at project completion/launch
- Example: $15,000 website = $7,500 at start + $7,500 at launch

## ROI Calculation Template

Use this formula for ROI projections:

Monthly Site Visitors x Conversion Rate = Leads
Leads x Close Rate = Jobs
Jobs x Average Job Value = Monthly Revenue
Monthly Revenue x 12 = Annual Revenue

### Default Assumptions (when client doesn't provide their own)
- Website conversion rate: 3-5% (conservative 3%, realistic 5%)
- Close rate on leads: 30-50% (conservative 30%, realistic 40%)
- Use industry-specific average job values when client doesn't specify
