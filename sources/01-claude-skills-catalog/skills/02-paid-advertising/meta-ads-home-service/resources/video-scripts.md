# Video Ad Scripts for Home Service Businesses

Video ads are the #1 proven format for home service Meta ads. Every script follows
the 6-Step Framework. Generate 3-5 variations per client using different angles.

---

## The 6-Step Video Ad Framework

Every owner intro video MUST hit these 6 elements in order:

| Step | Element | Purpose | Time |
|------|---------|---------|------|
| 1 | **Location callout** | Stop the scroll, pattern interrupt | 3-5 sec |
| 2 | **Who you are** | Humanize, introduce the owner | 3-5 sec |
| 3 | **What you do** | Explain the service clearly | 5-8 sec |
| 4 | **Why you're different** | Differentiate from competition | 8-12 sec |
| 5 | **Expiring value proposition** | Create urgency, drive action | 5-8 sec |
| 6 | **Strong CTA** | Tell them exactly what to click | 3-5 sec |

**Total target: 30-45 seconds. Never exceed 60 seconds.**

## Master Script Template

Use this as the base, then customize per client:

```
"What's up, [CITY/NEIGHBORHOOD] homeowners!

My name is [OWNER NAME]. I'm the owner of [BUSINESS NAME].

[We specialize in / All we do is] [PRIMARY SERVICE] for homeowners
right here in [AREA].

A few things that make us different:
- [Differentiator 1: licensing, certification, specialization]
- [Differentiator 2: experience, years in business, installs count]
- [Differentiator 3: background checks, warranty, locally owned]

Until the end of this month, the next [10/15/20] people that click
the [GET QUOTE / LEARN MORE] button right here will get [OFFER].

[Optional trust reinforcement: "Zero charge, zero upsells, zero anything."]

Click [GET QUOTE] right now to get started."
```

## Script Angles (Generate 3-5 Per Client)

### Angle 1: The Standard Owner Intro (Always Create This First)

The bread-and-butter. Owner introduces themselves in front of branded truck or
completed job. This is your "North Star" creative.

```
"Hey [CITY] homeowners!

My name's [NAME], owner of [BUSINESS].

We're a locally owned [TRADE] company and we've been serving [AREA]
for [X] years.

What makes us different:
- [All our techs are background checked and drug tested]
- [We're fully licensed, bonded, and insured]
- [We've got [X] five-star reviews]

Right now, the next [10] people to click the button below get
[OFFER — e.g., a free electrical safety inspection].

No strings attached. Click Get Quote now."
```

### Angle 2: The Problem Visual Hook

Owner holds up or points to a problem (melted outlet, rusty pipe, damaged roof).
Creates immediate curiosity.

```
"[CITY] homeowners — don't make this mistake."

[Hold up problem item or point to damage]

"My name's [NAME] with [BUSINESS]. I'm a licensed [TRADE] and I see
this [X] times a week.

[Explain the problem in 1-2 sentences]

If you're not sure whether your [system/home] has this issue, we'll
come out and do a [free inspection/consultation] — no charge, no pressure.

Click the link below and we'll get you on the schedule this week."
```

### Angle 3: The City Call-Out (Multi-Location)

Rapid-fire name multiple cities. Works great for businesses serving a wide area.

```
"[City 1]. [City 2]. [City 3]. [City 4].

If you own a home in any of these areas, listen up for 30 seconds.

My name's [NAME]. I own [BUSINESS] and we're the go-to [TRADE]
company for [SERVICE] in [REGION].

Here's what sets us apart:
- [Differentiator]
- [Differentiator]

Right now we're running a special — [OFFER] for homeowners in
these areas only.

Spots are limited. Click Get Quote before they're gone."
```

### Angle 4: The Just Got a [Thing] Angle

Targets people who recently made a related purchase (EV, new home, pool, etc.)

```
"Just got a [Tesla / new home / pool]?

My name's [NAME] with [BUSINESS]. We specialize in [SERVICE] and
we've done over [X] installs right here in [AREA].

Here's what most people don't know — [interesting fact about the service].

That's why we [key differentiator — e.g., pull permits, do load calculations,
use commercial-grade equipment].

Click below and we'll give you a [free consultation / $X off your install].

Don't wait — [seasonal/urgency reason]."
```

### Angle 5: The Warning / Education Hook

Position the owner as the expert warning against a common mistake.

```
"[CITY] homeowners — that [$X cheap option] could [consequence].

I'm [NAME], a licensed [TRADE] with [BUSINESS]. I've seen this [X] times.

When [common shortcut], here's what actually happens:
- [Consequence 1]
- [Consequence 2]

The right way costs [honest price range]. The wrong way costs [much more].

We do it right the first time. [Key trust points].

Click below for a [free estimate / consultation] — we'll walk you through
exactly what your home needs."
```

### Angle 6: The Neighbor Install / Social Proof

Film at a recently completed job site.

```
"We just finished [this install / this project] right here in [NEIGHBORHOOD].

[Show the completed work]

I'm [NAME] with [BUSINESS]. This homeowner called us because [reason],
and we [what you did — e.g., upgraded their panel, installed their charger].

[If applicable: "Check out the before and after"]

If you want something similar for your home, click the button below
and we'll get you a [free estimate / $X off].

We've got [X] five-star reviews and we're fully [licensed/insured/bonded]."
```

## Testimonial Interview Framework

After the client has completed jobs (usually 30+ days into running ads),
create customer testimonial videos.

### How to Film

1. Owner introduces the business briefly (5-10 seconds)
2. Cut to on-camera interview with happy customer
3. Ask these questions naturally:
   - "Why did you decide to get this done?"
   - "How was the experience working with us?"
   - "What was your favorite part?"
   - "What would you tell other homeowners thinking about this?"
4. Let the customer talk naturally — don't script them
5. End with CTA (owner spoken or on-screen text)

### Why It Works

Third-party validation is more believable than self-promotion. When a viewer
sees someone in their same market who paid and is happy, it resonates harder
than any amount of self-promotion.

### Best Testimonial Elements (What to Listen For)

- Customer mentions specific results ("spa vibe," "my sons felt like family")
- Customer highlights communication speed ("got back to us within minutes")
- Customer mentions unique value ("only company that offered X")
- Natural, unscripted speech patterns feel authentic

## Filming Checklist

### Equipment (Budget-Friendly)
| Item | Cost | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| iPhone (any recent model) | Already own | Better camera than Hollywood 25 years ago |
| Lapel microphone (Amazon) | $50-300 | Plugs into phone. Audio quality is non-negotiable |
| Tripod (Amazon) | $30-35 | Optional but professional |
| CapCut (app) | Free | For editing, B-roll, subtitles |

### What to Wear
- Branded polo with embroidered logo (required)
- Clean, professional appearance
- Film in front of branded/wrapped vehicle when possible
- The test: "Would a stay-at-home mom be comfortable with you showing up?"

### Where to Film
- In front of branded vehicle (strongest trust signal)
- At a completed job site
- In front of the business/office
- On an active job (B-roll)
- Never in a messy/unprofessional setting

### Production Rules
- All videos MUST have subtitles (85% watch on mute)
- Add B-roll of completed work when possible
- Location name as on-screen text in first 3 seconds
- Keep it conversational — don't read from a teleprompter feel
- Film 3-5 takes and pick the best one
- Film the same script in different locations for variation testing

---

## Creative Variation Strategy

When you find a winning script:
1. Keep the exact same script
2. Change the setting (truck, job site, office, completed project)
3. Test which variable matters — was it the script or the setting?
4. Double down on the winner
5. Create more variations of the winner, not new scripts

Variation examples for one winning script:
- Owner in front of branded vehicle
- Owner on job site mid-install
- Owner in front of completed project
- Crew member delivering the same script
- Same script with different B-roll footage
