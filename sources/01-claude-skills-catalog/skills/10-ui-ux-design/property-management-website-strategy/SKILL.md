# Property Management Website Strategy

Systematic approach to creating high-converting property management websites that serve dual audiences (property owners + tenants) while establishing clear competitive differentiation.

## Core Workflow

Follow this sequence for all property management website projects:

### Phase 1: Discovery & Research

1. **Administer Questionnaire**
   - Use [questionnaire-template.md](references/questionnaire-template.md) as your guide
   - If client has completed a questionnaire, process it thoroughly
   - If starting from scratch, ask questions iteratively (3-5 at a time to avoid overwhelming)
   - Document all responses in a structured format

2. **Process Strategy Call Transcript** (if available)
   - Extract client's voice: How do they describe their service? What words do they use?
   - Identify pain points: What problems do they solve? What frustrates their customers?
   - Capture competitive intelligence: Who do they compete with? What makes them different?
   - Note specific claims: Numbers, timeframes, guarantees, processes
   - Flag messaging themes: Recurring phrases or concepts that define their brand
   
   **Key extraction points:**
   - Unique value propositions
   - Client objections and how they're handled
   - Process steps (consultation → placement → management)
   - Guarantees or warranties offered
   - Pricing structure and transparency
   - Technology/systems used
   - Team background and credentials

3. **Conduct Competitor Research**
   - Follow methodology in [competitor-research.md](references/competitor-research.md)
   - Analyze 3-5 direct competitors mentioned by client
   - Search: `[city] property management companies` and `best property management websites`
   - Document for each competitor:
     - Homepage structure and sections
     - Messaging themes and claims
     - Unique features or guarantees
     - Strengths to learn from
     - Weaknesses to differentiate against
   - Identify industry patterns (sections that appear on 3+ sites)
   - Spot differentiation opportunities (gaps in competitor messaging)

### Phase 2: Strategic Planning

4. **Extract Core Messaging Themes**
   
   Based on questionnaire and transcript, identify:
   
   **Brand Personality** (3 descriptive words)
   - Example: Boutique, Hands-On, Trustworthy
   
   **Key Differentiators** (3-5 specific advantages)
   - Example: "One point of contact vs department runaround"
   - Example: "10 days to rent vs 30-60 days"
   - Example: "Flat rate pricing vs hidden fees"
   
   **Pain Points Addressed** (3-5 owner frustrations)
   - Example: "Property managers who don't return calls"
   - Example: "Tenants sitting without paying for months"
   - Example: "Surprise fees you never agreed to"
   
   **Value Propositions** (3 main promises)
   - Speed claim (days to rent, response time)
   - Quality claim (tenant screening, service level)
   - Transparency claim (pricing, communication)
   
   **Proof Elements** (credibility builders)
   - Years in business
   - Number of properties managed
   - Eviction rate or tenant retention stats
   - Guarantees offered
   - Credentials (broker license, certifications)

5. **Determine Section Architecture**
   
   Consult [section-architecture.md](references/section-architecture.md) for proven structures.
   
   **Standard Homepage Sections** (adjust based on client needs):
   1. Navigation (with portals/payment links)
   2. Hero (dual audience: owners + tenants)
   3. Quick Links (service shortcuts)
   4. Value Propositions (3 promises)
   5. Featured Rentals (tenant-focused, owner proof)
   6. Comparison Table (us vs competitors)
   7. Services Overview (6-8 core services)
   8. Guarantee/Risk Reversal
   9. How It Works (4-7 step process)
   10. Service Areas (cities/neighborhoods)
   11. Testimonials (3-4 owner/tenant quotes)
   12. Meet Team/About (founder story, credentials)
   13. Final Dual CTA (owners + tenants)
   14. Footer (contact, links, legal)
   
   **Decision Points:**
   - Owner-heavy vs tenant-heavy vs balanced approach?
   - Should rentals be featured prominently or secondary?
   - Is comparison table appropriate (boutique vs corporate)?
   - How detailed should process section be?
   - Single CTA or dual CTA throughout?

### Phase 3: Wireframe Creation

6. **Create Initial Wireframe**
   
   Build section-by-section structure:
   
   **For Each Section:**
   - Section name/label (for internal use)
   - Purpose statement (what this section accomplishes)
   - Content outline (headlines, body copy placeholders, CTA text)
   - Layout notes (columns, cards, grids, etc.)
   
   **Wireframe Format:**
   - Text-based outline OR
   - Simple HTML with minimal styling OR
   - Structured document (docx/md)
   
   **First draft goals:**
   - Establish information hierarchy
   - Map content flow from awareness → consideration → decision
   - Ensure dual audience needs are met
   - Create clear conversion pathways

7. **Write Conversion-Focused Copy**
   
   Apply principles from [copywriting-principles.md](references/copywriting-principles.md):
   
   **Headlines:**
   - Use proven formulas (PAS, Before-After-Bridge, FAB)
   - Lead with benefits, not features
   - Include specific claims when possible
   
   **Body Copy:**
   - Address pain points explicitly
   - Use concrete numbers and timeframes
   - Translate features into benefits
   - Include social proof strategically
   - Speak to "you" (the visitor) not "we" (the company)
   
   **CTAs:**
   - Action-oriented verbs
   - Low-commitment language ("free," "no obligation")
   - Clarify what happens next
   - Use multiple CTAs for different stages of readiness
   
   **Voice & Tone:**
   - Match client's natural speaking style (from transcript)
   - Professional but warm for owners
   - Helpful and clear for tenants
   - Confident without arrogance
   
   **Specificity Checklist:**
   - Replace "fast" with "10 days or less"
   - Replace "experienced" with "25+ years"
   - Replace "quality tenants" with "verified credit, criminal, employment"
   - Replace "great service" with "same-day response"

### Phase 4: Review & Iteration

8. **Cross-Check Against Source Material**
   
   **Verification Process:**
   - Compare wireframe copy against questionnaire answers
   - Cross-reference claims in transcript with copy
   - Ensure competitor research insights are incorporated
   - Validate that all key differentiators are highlighted
   
   **Common Discrepancies to Catch:**
   - Pricing (check for all tiers, not just base rate)
   - Timing (days to rent, response time, etc.)
   - Guarantees (exact terms and conditions)
   - Process steps (accurate sequence and details)
   - Service areas (complete list, not just major cities)
   - Credentials (correct license numbers, years in business)
   
   **What to Look For:**
   - Missing value propositions
   - Vague claims that could be more specific
   - Contradictions between sections
   - Opportunities to strengthen messaging
   - Sections that don't serve a clear purpose

9. **Create Deliverables**
   
   **High-Fidelity Wireframe:**
   - Polished copy for all sections
   - Visual hierarchy indicated
   - Detailed content for designer reference
   - Includes notes/annotations where helpful
   - Format: HTML with styling, or detailed document
   
   **Low-Fidelity Wireframe:**
   - Same content, simplified presentation
   - Remove section labels visible to designer
   - Use placeholder boxes [IMAGE], [PHOTO], etc.
   - Minimal or no styling (prevents design copying)
   - Black/white/gray only
   - Simple borders and typography
   - Format: Clean HTML or basic document
   
   **Supporting Documents:**
   - Completed questionnaire (if created during process)
   - Competitor research summary
   - Messaging themes document
   - Content inventory/checklist

## Critical Success Factors

### Dual Audience Balance
Property management websites must serve two distinct audiences:
- **Property Owners** need persuasion (why hire us?)
- **Tenants** need information (what's available? how do I apply?)

Never sacrifice one audience for the other. Use dual CTAs, separate sections where appropriate, and ensure both journeys are frictionless.

### Specificity Over Vagueness
Generic claims ("excellent service," "fast placement") mean nothing. Always push for:
- Exact timeframes ("10 days or less")
- Concrete numbers ("Only 3 evictions out of 350 properties last year")
- Specific processes ("Credit + criminal + employment + income + references")
- Measurable guarantees ("One year or next placement free")

### Differentiation Through Contrast
Property management is commoditized. Stand out by:
- **Naming the pain:** "Put in a call Monday, lucky to hear back Friday"
- **Showing the contrast:** "Other companies vs us"
- **Being specific:** "One point of contact" not "great communication"
- **Proving it:** Guarantees, testimonials, statistics

### Conversion Psychology
Apply proven principles:
- **Social proof:** Years in business, # properties, client tenure, testimonials
- **Risk reversal:** Guarantees, free consultations, no long-term contracts
- **Authority:** Credentials, experience, local expertise
- **Scarcity:** Limited availability, filling properties fast
- **Reciprocity:** Free resources, market analysis, consultation

## When to Deviate from Standard Structure

The 14-section homepage structure is proven but not rigid. Adjust when:

**Client is highly specialized:**
- Luxury property management → Emphasize white-glove service
- Student housing → Tenant-focused, different pain points
- HOA/condo management → Different services, different audience

**Market conditions differ:**
- Highly competitive rental market → Feature rentals prominently
- Low inventory, high demand → Owner-focused messaging
- Tight urban market → Neighborhood specificity matters more

**Company is very small or very large:**
- Solo operator → Personal story, direct access to owner
- Large company → Systems, scale, consistency, technology

**Unique business model:**
- Flat-fee only (no percentage) → Emphasize cost savings
- Placement-only (no ongoing management) → Different guarantees
- Full-service + à la carte → Flexible options

## Quality Checks

Before delivering wireframes, verify:

✅ Both audiences (owners + tenants) have clear pathways
✅ All claims are specific and provable
✅ Competitive differentiation is obvious
✅ CTAs are present, clear, and varied
✅ Pain points are addressed explicitly
✅ Guarantees or risk reversals are included
✅ Social proof is strategically placed
✅ Copy matches client's voice (from transcript)
✅ No contradictions between sections
✅ Mobile users can complete key actions
✅ Contact information is prominent
✅ SEO fundamentals are present (service areas, keywords)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Treating all PM companies the same:** Each has unique positioning
❌ **Forgetting the tenant audience:** They drive revenue too
❌ **Being vague:** "Fast" and "quality" mean nothing without specifics
❌ **Copying competitors:** Stand out by being different, not similar
❌ **Overcomplicating:** Simple, clear messaging beats clever wordplay
❌ **Hiding pricing:** Transparency builds trust
❌ **No clear next step:** Every section should guide toward action
❌ **Ignoring mobile:** 60%+ of traffic is mobile

## Example Project Flow

1. Client uploads strategy call transcript
2. Review transcript, extract key themes
3. Use questionnaire template to identify gaps
4. Search for 3-5 competitors, document findings
5. Identify client's unique positioning (boutique vs corporate)
6. Map out 14-section homepage structure
7. Write copy for each section (high-fidelity)
8. Review against transcript for accuracy
9. Create low-fidelity version for designer
10. Present both versions with strategic rationale

## Tips for Working with Transcripts

Strategy call transcripts are goldmines. Mine them for:

**Direct Quotes:**
- How the client naturally describes their service
- Stories they tell (these become case studies/testimonials)
- Objections they address (become FAQ items)
- Phrases they repeat (become messaging themes)

**Implied Insights:**
- What they spend time on = what matters most
- What frustrates them = industry pain points
- How they compare themselves = differentiation strategy
- What they're proud of = credibility builders

**Red Flags:**
- Contradictions (clarify before proceeding)
- Vague answers (dig deeper with follow-up questions)
- Missing information (add to questionnaire)
- Unrealistic claims (verify or soften)
