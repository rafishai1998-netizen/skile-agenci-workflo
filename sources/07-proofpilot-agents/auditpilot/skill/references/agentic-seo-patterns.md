# Agentic SEO Patterns Adapted for AuditPilot

Use this reference when you want to sharpen AuditPilot with deeper evidence collection and more complete coverage, without turning the audit into a generic technical checklist.

## Purpose

AuditPilot is still a strategic, sales-oriented audit skill.
This reference adds useful support layers from LLM-first SEO workflows so the audit is:
- more evidence-backed
- more complete
- better at catching basic misses
- stronger on AI-search and answer-engine readiness
- better at avoiding overclaiming

These patterns support the main narrative. They do not replace the ranking, market, competitor, and conversion story.

## Embedded Audit Roles

Think through these specialist roles when collecting and synthesizing evidence:

1. **Technical baseline reviewer**
   Checks crawlability, robots, redirects, broken links, render issues, and core technical misses.

2. **Content quality reviewer**
   Checks thin content, duplication, E-E-A-T gaps, readability, weak proof, and generic AI-sounding copy.

3. **Image and media reviewer**
   Checks missing alt text, oversized files, wrong formats, missing dimensions, poor lazy-loading choices, and weak gallery image SEO.

4. **Internal linking reviewer**
   Checks weak hub-and-spoke structure, orphan-like important pages, poor service-to-location linking, and dead-end proof pages.

5. **Schema and entity reviewer**
   Checks structured data quality, entity clarity, sameAs signals, brand consistency, and trust-impacting mismatches.

6. **GEO and AEO reviewer**
   Checks AI-search support layers such as llms.txt, AI crawler access, answer-block structure, comparison-page opportunities, quotable passages, and entity/citation opportunities.

7. **Visual proof reviewer**
   Checks whether the site has the visuals and proof assets to support the pages it should be building, like project photos, before-and-after assets, team proof, review proof, and location proof.

8. **Verifier**
   Dedupe overlapping findings, remove contradictions, and downgrade unsupported claims.

## Evidence Format

For major findings, support them with:
- **Finding**
- **Evidence**
- **Impact**
- **Fix**

Use confidence labels internally when helpful:
- **Confirmed** — directly observed
- **Likely** — strong signal, partial verification
- **Hypothesis** — plausible but needs more proof

Do not turn likely or hypothesis-grade findings into client-facing certainty.

## Best Support Layers To Borrow

### 1. Image audit layer
Useful checks:
- missing alt text
- keyword-stuffed or useless alt text
- oversized files
- wrong formats where lighter formats would help
- missing width/height dimensions
- weak lazy-loading strategy
- poor image filenames
- weak project/gallery image optimization

**When it matters most:** visual industries, project-heavy sites, ecommerce, design-led brands.

### 2. Link and architecture layer
Useful checks:
- orphan-like important pages
- weak internal links into money pages
- broken links
- redirect chains
- poor hub-to-spoke support
- proof pages disconnected from service/location pages

**When it matters most:** larger sites, redesigns, sites with many services or cities.

### 3. GEO layer
Useful checks:
- robots rules affecting AI crawlers
- llms.txt presence when relevant
- pages with strong quotable passages
- opportunities for AI-citable pages like best-of, how-to-choose, vs, alternatives, or top-rated pages
- entity clarity and source-supporting structure

**Use this to strengthen strategy and positioning, not to create AI hype.**

### 4. AEO layer
Useful checks:
- question-based headings
- concise direct-answer blocks
- featured snippet style formatting
- FAQ structuring where appropriate
- knowledge-panel support signals
- SearchAction or answer-optimized page patterns when relevant

**When it matters most:** high-information, comparison-heavy, or trust-heavy categories.

### 5. Content quality layer
Useful checks:
- thin pages
- duplicated city or service pages
- generic AI-sounding copy
- weak expertise signals
- lack of proof, specifics, pricing guidance, process detail, or examples
- low passage quality for AI citation and answer extraction

### 6. Verifier layer
Before finalizing the audit:
- merge duplicate findings
- remove contradictions
- downgrade unsupported claims
- avoid calling environment limits site defects
- make sure the finding still matters to the audit story

## How To Use This In AuditPilot

### Good use
- Strengthen the audit with sharper support evidence
- Catch obvious misses other providers left behind
- Surface AI-search and answer-engine opportunities when relevant
- Give Matthew stronger audit depth without losing the strategic story

### Bad use
- Turning the audit into a 40-point technical checklist
- Leading with alt text before ranking reality
- burying the business story under scripts and metrics
- treating every support-layer issue as equally important

## Prioritization Rule

Keep the main hierarchy:
1. ranking reality
2. competitor advantage
3. page-system and content gaps
4. credibility killers / red flags
5. revenue and growth implications
6. support-layer misses that strengthen the case

Support layers should sharpen the argument, not replace it.
