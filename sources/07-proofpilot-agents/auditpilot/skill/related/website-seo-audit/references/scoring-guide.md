## Traffic Scoring

### 1A: Organic Traffic & Rankings

| Score | Criteria | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 0-2 | Zero or near-zero organic traffic | Site has 0-50 monthly visitors |
| 3-4 | Minimal traffic, declining | Under 500/mo, mostly from branded searches |
| 5-6 | Moderate traffic, stable | 500-2,000/mo with some keyword rankings |
| 7-8 | Strong traffic, growing | 2,000-5,000/mo with diverse keywords |
| 9-10 | Dominant traffic | 5,000+/mo with top positions for money keywords |

**Important:** Quality matters more than volume. A site with 500 local visitors who convert beats 10,000 national blog readers who never call.

**What to write in notes:**
- State traffic number and trend (up/down/flat over 6 months)
- Identify traffic sources (organic, direct, referral, paid)
- Note if traffic is local (valuable) or national (often worthless for local businesses)

### 1B: On-Page SEO Score

| Score | BrightLocal Score | Issues |
|-------|-------------------|--------|
| 0-3 | Under 40 | Missing titles, no descriptions, no schema |
| 4-5 | 40-59 | Some pages optimized, many missing |
| 6-7 | 60-74 | Most pages have basics, minor issues |
| 8 | 75-89 | Well optimized with minor gaps |
| 9-10 | 90+ | Fully optimized across all pages |

**What to write in notes:**
- State the score
- List specific issues: "Missing meta descriptions on 4 pages. No local business schema. Title tags generic and missing city names."

### 1C: Link Profile

| Score | Referring Domains | Quality |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| 0-3 | Under 10 | Few or spammy links |
| 4-5 | 10-30 | Mostly directories |
| 6-7 | 30-75 | Mix of directories and legitimate sites |
| 8 | 75-150 | Quality domains including local/industry |
| 9-10 | 150+ | Strong profile with authoritative links |

**What to write in notes:**
- State referring domain count
- Note quality: "Most links from low-quality directories. Missing links from local chambers, industry associations, suppliers, and news sites."

### 1D: Citations

| Score | Citation Count | NAP Consistency |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|
| 0-3 | Under 20 | Major inconsistencies or missing |
| 4-5 | 20-40 | Several NAP issues |
| 6-7 | 40-60 | Mostly consistent, minor issues |
| 8 | 60-80 | Clean citations, well managed |
| 9-10 | 80+ | Comprehensive and perfectly consistent |

**What to write in notes:**
- State citation count
- Note specific inconsistencies: "Phone number wrong on 6 listings. Old address showing on Yelp and Yellow Pages."

---

### 1E: Google Business Profile

| Score | Profile State | Activity |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| 0-3 | Incomplete or unclaimed | No posts, minimal info |
| 4-5 | Claimed, basics filled | Sporadic posts, missing photos |
| 6-7 | Good profile | Regular posts, decent photos |
| 8-9 | Fully optimized | Consistent posts, Q&A managed, products/services complete |
| 10 | Perfect | Dominating local pack, excellent engagement |

**What to write in notes:**
- List what's missing: categories, services, products, photos, posts
- Name competitors ranking above them in local pack
- Example: "GBP claimed but not optimized. No products listed. Posts are generic AI content. Competitors Code 3 Junk Removal and Junk King ranking above for 'junk removal phoenix.'"

### 1F: Local Pack Visibility

| Score | Heatmap Result |
|-------|----------------|
| 0-2 | All red/gray across service area |
| 3-4 | Mostly red with some yellow |
| 5-6 | Mix of yellow and green |

| 9-10 | Dominating green across entire service area |

**What to write in notes:**
- Describe what you see on the heatmap

## Trust Scoring

### 2A: Risk Reversal / Guarantee

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | No guarantee or risk reversal mentioned anywhere |
| 3-4 | Weak guarantee buried in fine print |
| 5-6 | Guarantee present but not prominent |
| 7-8 | Clear guarantee visible on homepage |
| 9-10 | Strong, differentiated guarantee as competitive advantage |

**Examples of good risk reversal:**
- "100% Satisfaction Guarantee"
- "No Surprises Pricing - You'll know the cost before we start"
- "Same-Day Service or It's Free"
- "Lifetime Warranty on All Work"

---

### 2B: Reviews & Social Proof

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | No reviews displayed on website |
| 3-4 | Few reviews, no star ratings shown |
| 5-6 | Some reviews/testimonials present |
| 7-8 | Strong review display with counts, stars, real names |
| 9-10 | Dominant social proof (video testimonials, platform widgets, counts) |

**What makes a 9-10:**
- Google review widget with live reviews
- Review count prominently displayed ("127 Five-Star Reviews")
- Video testimonials from real customers
- Reviews from multiple platforms visible
- Testimonials with full names and photos

---

### 2C: Trust Badges & Reputation

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | No trust signals visible |
| 3-4 | License number only |
| 5-6 | Basic "Licensed & Insured" badge |
| 7-8 | Multiple trust signals (license, insurance, BBB, certifications) |
| 9-10 | Comprehensive trust display (awards, manufacturer certs, industry recognition) |

**Trust signals to look for:**
- License number (not just "Licensed")
- Insurance verification
- BBB Accredited badge
- Industry certifications (NATE, IBEW, etc.)
- Manufacturer certifications (Tesla Certified, etc.)
- Awards (Best of Yelp, Angi Super Service, etc.)

---

### 2D: Personality & Face

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | No photos or only stock images |
| 3-4 | Generic team mention, no real photos |
| 5-6 | Some real photos but not prominent |
| 7-8 | Owner visible on homepage, team photos |
| 9-10 | Strong personal brand (owner story, team profiles, behind-scenes content) |

**What makes a 9-10:**
- Owner photo on homepage with name
- Full team page with individual photos and bios
- Photos of actual work trucks (not stock)
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Video introductions

---

### 2E: "Like, Know & Trust" Factor

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-3 | Cold, corporate, impersonal tone |
| 4-5 | Neutral, neither warm nor cold |
| 6-7 | Somewhat friendly and approachable |
| 8-9 | Personable, relatable, human feel |
| 10 | Highly relatable, feels like a friend recommending them |

**Indicators of high Like/Know/Trust:**
- Conversational copy (not corporate speak)
- Community involvement highlighted
- Family business feel
- Local roots emphasized
- Personality shows through the content

---

### 2F: Emotional Backstory / Mission

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | No story or mission anywhere |
| 3-4 | Generic "quality service" about page |
| 5-6 | Some company history, but not compelling |
| 7-8 | Clear mission, founder story, values |
| 9-10 | Compelling narrative that builds connection |

**What makes a compelling story:**
- Why the owner started the business
- What problem they saw and wanted to fix
- Family or community connection
- Personal mission beyond profit
- Values that resonate with customers

## Conversion Scoring

### 3A: Calls to Action

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | No clear way to contact |
| 3-4 | Contact info buried, no CTAs |
| 5-6 | Basic contact page, few CTAs |
| 7-8 | Phone in header, CTAs on each page |
| 9-10 | Optimized CTAs throughout, multiple contact options |

**Optimal CTA setup:**
- Phone number in header (click-to-call on mobile)
- "Get Quote" button in header
- CTA above the fold on every page
- Multiple contact options (call, form, text, chat)
- Sticky mobile CTA bar

---

### 3B: Incentive to Act Now

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-3 | No urgency or incentive |
| 4-5 | Weak incentive ("Contact us today") |
| 6-7 | Some urgency (seasonal mention) |
| 8-9 | Clear incentive with deadline |
| 10 | Compelling offer with genuine urgency |

**Examples of good incentives:**
- "$50 Off Your First Service - This Month Only"
- "Free Safety Inspection with Any Repair"
- "Same-Day Service Available"
- "Financing Available - 0% for 12 Months"

---

### 3C: Mobile Responsiveness

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | Broken on mobile, unusable |
| 3-5 | Functional but poor experience |
| 6-7 | Works well, minor issues |
| 8-9 | Excellent mobile experience |
| 10 | Mobile-first design, perfect UX |

**Test on actual mobile device:**
- Can you tap the phone number to call?
- Is text readable without zooming?
- Are buttons large enough to tap?
- Does the form work properly?
- Is navigation accessible?

---

### 3D: Page Speed

| Score | Load Time | PageSpeed Score |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| 0-2 | 5+ seconds | Under 30 |
| 3-4 | 3-5 seconds | 30-49 |
| 5-6 | 2-3 seconds | 50-69 |
| 7-8 | 1-2 seconds | 70-89 |
| 9-10 | Under 1 second | 90+ |

---

### 3E: Form Friction

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-3 | Complex form, 10+ fields, confusing |
| 4-5 | Too many required fields |
| 6-7 | Reasonable fields, could be simpler |
| 8-9 | Simple form (name, phone, brief message) |
| 10 | Optimized (name + phone only, or chat option) |

## SEO & Content Scoring

### 4A: Page Inventory

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | Only homepage exists |
| 3-4 | Homepage + contact, maybe 1 service page |
| 5-6 | Basic service pages, no locations |
| 7-8 | Good service coverage, some locations |
| 9-10 | Comprehensive: all services, all locations, combos |

**Check against GBP categories:** Do they have a page for each service category listed in their GBP?

---

### 4B: URL Structure

| Score | Criteria | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 0-3 | Random/messy URLs | /page?id=123 |
| 4-5 | Inconsistent structure | Mix of formats |
| 6-7 | Decent, mostly clean | /services/electrical |
| 8-9 | Clean and logical | /services/panel-upgrade |
| 10 | Perfect hierarchy | /mesa-az/panel-upgrade |

---

### 4C: Service Page Quality

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-3 | Thin content (<200 words) or duplicate |
| 4-5 | Basic content, generic |
| 5-6 | Adequate (300-500 words), some unique elements |
| 7-8 | Strong (500+ words), unique, well-structured |
| 9-10 | Excellent (1000+ words), FAQs, images, CTAs, schema |

---

### 4D: Location Pages

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | No location pages |
| 3-4 | Only one location mentioned |
| 5-6 | Few city pages |
| 7-8 | Most cities covered |
| 9-10 | All cities + service+city combo pages |

---

### 4E: Blog/Content Quality

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | No blog OR spam content (unrelated topics) |
| 3-4 | Generic AI content, no value |
| 5-6 | Basic helpful content |
| 7-8 | Quality content with expertise |
| 9-10 | Authoritative content (unique insights, local focus, video) |

**Red flags:**
- Blog posts about topics unrelated to the business (spam)
- Obviously AI-generated with no customization
- Content that doesn't match their services
- Generic advice that could apply to any business

---

### 4F: FAQ Quality

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | No FAQs |
| 3-4 | Generic FAQs not matching actual questions |
| 5-6 | Some relevant FAQs |
| 7-8 | Good FAQs matching People Also Ask |
| 9-10 | Comprehensive FAQs with schema markup |

**Test:** Search their primary keyword. Look at People Also Ask. Do their FAQs answer those questions?

---

### 4G: Technical SEO

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0-3 | Missing basics (no meta titles/descriptions) |
| 4-5 | Partial implementation |
| 6-7 | Most elements present |
| 8-9 | Well optimized |
| 10 | Perfect (unique titles/descriptions, schema, sitemap, fast) |

**Check for:**
- Unique meta titles with keywords and city
- Unique meta descriptions
- Schema markup (LocalBusiness, FAQ, Review)
- XML sitemap submitted
- No indexing issues
