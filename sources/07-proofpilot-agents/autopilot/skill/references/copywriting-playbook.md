# Copywriting Playbook — Home Service Voice + Conversion Copy

> How every WebsitePilot demo ships copy that converts. Paired with `home-service-blueprint.md` (the strategy) + `seo-playbook.md` (the rank layer) — this doc is the **voice** and **conversion-copy** layer.
>
> Every demo must pass the Copy Checklist at the end of this doc before Stage 7 deploy.

---

## The copy premise

People are busy, burned before, and scrolling on their phone between jobs. Your copy has to do one thing: **make them trust you enough to pick up the phone**. Everything else is noise.

Rules:

1. **Second-person, never third.** "We fix leaks" not "Our company provides leak repair services."
2. **Specifics > adjectives.** "127 Five-Star Reviews" beats "Highly Rated." "$85 flat rate" beats "Affordable."
3. **Numbers before everything.** If a claim can be a number, lead with the number.
4. **One idea per sentence.** Short. Complete. Scannable.
5. **Plainspoken-trade > corporate.** "We show up when we say we will" beats "Punctual service delivery commitment."
6. **Lead with the decision.** Put the point first. Supporting context second.
7. **Active voice, active verbs.** "We'll haul it" not "It will be hauled."

## Hero copy formulas (7 proven variants)

Use one of these or a close variant. Don't freestyle the hero — it's the 3-second window.

### Formula 1 — SEO-keyword-led

> `#1 [Service] in [City]`
> `[Service] in [City] — [USP in 4-6 words]`
> `[Benefit sentence. One line. Period.]`

Example: `Scorpion Hunters. Termite Evictors. Family-Owned in Mesa since 2011.`

### Formula 2 — founder-voice origin-story

> `[Evocative noun + noun. Two- or three-word declarative.]`
> `[One-sentence why this company exists, founder-voice.]`

Example (Richardson Pest): `Scorpions are out tonight. We'll be out tomorrow.` / `Family-owned East Valley pest control. Nelson Richardson started this company after his 2-year-old was stung.`

### Formula 3 — problem-statement

> `[Problem]? [Our answer — verb-forward].`
> `[Credibility + timeline line.]`

Example: `Garage full of junk? We'll haul it today.` / `Licensed, insured, and out the door same-day across the East Valley. $85 flat rate.`

### Formula 4 — title-case editorial (dfw-luxe-aerial / premium-design-build)

> `[Title-case headline, 4-6 words, ONE noun color-painted in accent.]`
> `[Subhead in Work Sans / Inter, 2 lines max.]`

Example (Anomaly Pool): `We Make Backyards You Never Want to Leave.` / "Dallas-Fort Worth pool design-build. Real portfolio. Real clients."

### Formula 5 — italic editorial (editorial-serif)

> `[Roman headline] [italic accent phrase] [roman continuation].`
> `[Restrained subhead.]`

Example (Kingswood): `Designed with intention. Built to last.` / "Bespoke landscape design-build for DFW estates. By appointment."

### Formula 6 — archetype-mascot declaration (180-Sites style)

> `WE ARE THE [MASCOT NAMES].` (ALL CAPS, stacked)
> `[One-sentence what we do + city.]`

Example (Volt Vikings): `POWER YOUR HOME.` / "Mesa's only Viking-owned electrical contractor. Licensed, insured, here to conquer circuits."

### Formula 7 — split-testing tagline

> `#1 [Service] in [City]` (SEO tagline on top, small eyebrow)
> `[Big declarative headline — can be informal, sharp, founder-voice]`
> `[Subhead: what + how + proof in 12-18 words]`

Example: "#1 Pressure Washing in Orlando" (eyebrow) / `Your house shouldn't look tired.` / "Same-day soft-wash + pressure washing. Licensed, insured, $149 starting. 4.9 ⭐ from 312 Orlando homeowners."

## Voice register per preset

| Preset | Voice register | Tone DON'T | Example opener |
|--------|---------------|------------|----------------|
| `rugged-industrial` | Plainspoken-trade, direct, confident, blue-collar pride | Don't be corporate, don't use adjectives, don't soften | "We haul junk. Same day. Done." |
| `archetype-mascot` | Declaration-forward, fun-but-serious, ALL CAPS OK | Don't be actually childish | "WE ARE THE VIKINGS. THE ELECTRICAL KIND." |
| `contractor-heritage` | Professional-operator, credibility-first, heritage-proud | Don't be too casual, this is trust-heavy work | "Dallas plumbing since 1987. Licensed, insured, here when you call." |
| `dfw-luxe-aerial` | Aspirational-clean, confident understatement | Don't be scarcity-marketer, don't beg for the call | "We build backyards you never want to leave." |
| `playful-chunky-consumer` | Character-led, warm, personal, micro-jokes OK | Don't be boring, but don't over-joke trust moments | "Your lights, our Santa hats. Same energy, better installation." |
| `editorial-serif` | Measured, authoritative, warm-but-restrained | Don't be clinical, don't be salesy | "Preventive dentistry designed around your family's rhythm." |
| `premium-design-build` | Craft-forward, portfolio-led, understated confidence | Don't be loud, don't list prices | "Every project begins with a conversation about what's possible." |

## CTA copy library — what the button says

Match the CTA to the preset + vertical + funnel stage. These convert.

| Situation | Button copy | Secondary CTA |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Most home service (universal) | `Get A Free Quote` | `Call [phone]` |
| Urgent/same-day | `Schedule Same-Day Service` | `Call Now — We Answer` |
| Roofing/financing | `Start Your Free Estimate` | `See Financing Options` |
| Plumbing/HVAC emergency | `Request Service` | `24/7 Emergency: [phone]` |
| Consultative / design-build | `Start a Project` OR `Begin the Conversation` | `Book a Site Visit` |
| Premium editorial (dental/medical) | `Request an Appointment` | `Call Our Office` |
| Archetype-mascot bold | `GET A QUOTE` (all caps) | `CALL [phone]` |
| Pest/recurring plan | `Schedule a Quote` | `Free Inspection` |
| Pressure washing / cleaning | `Get Your Instant Quote` | `Text a Photo` |
| Landscape/hardscape portfolio | `Start Your Project` | `View Our Work` |

**Avoid:**
- "Learn More" (too vague, weak intent)
- "Click Here" (obvious, weak)
- "Submit" (forgettable form button)
- "Contact Us" (better than Learn More, still weak vs "Get A Free Quote")

## Benefits-not-features — the 5×5 rule

Every "Why You" section ships with 3-5 items. Each item is a BENEFIT that counters a specific customer FEAR. Never features.

| Fear | Feature (wrong) | Benefit (right) |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| "They'll nickel-and-dime me" | "Detailed invoicing" | `Upfront pricing. What we quote is what you pay.` |
| "They won't show up" | "Punctual service" | `Same-day service. If we're not there by the window, the job's free.` |
| "They'll screw up my house" | "Insured & bonded" | `Licensed #9626, fully insured. Your homeowner's policy never touches us.` |
| "I'll get the bait-and-switch" | "Honest pricing" | `Flat-rate. No surprises. You see the total before we swing a hammer.` |
| "What if I don't like the work?" | "Quality guarantee" | `Not happy? We come back free until you are. No questions.` |
| "These guys will disappear after the job" | "Long-established" | `Family-owned since 2008. We live in the same zip code you do.` |

## Section-by-section copy templates

### Hero
Already covered in the 7 formulas above.

### Before/After
Each case study caption: `[City]: [What was wrong]. [What we did].` — 2 sentences max.

Example: `Mesa: 25-year-old roof leaking in three places. Full tear-off + GAF Timberline shingle install. 2-day turnaround.`

### Reviews
Lift direct quotes. Keep short. Show first name + city. Show stars + platform icon.

Example row: `"Showed up at 8am like they said, done by 1. Clean workers, clean yard. Top tier."` — **Jen R., Chandler** · ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Google

### Trust Numbers
`[Big Number][Short Label]` — keep each stat 2-4 words.

- `18+` / `Years in Mesa`
- `1,287` / `Five-Star Reviews`
- `3 Generations` / `of Cullimore Dentistry`
- `$85` / `Flat Rate Starting`

### Why You
Benefits-not-features, 5 max, each 6-14 words with a concrete number or guarantee.

### How It Works
3-4 steps, each 3-6 words:
1. `Call or fill out the form`
2. `Free estimate within 24 hours`
3. `We do the work`
4. `Final walkthrough with you`

### Services
Each service: one-sentence description.

Example: `Scorpion Control — Interior + perimeter treatment. Pet-safe pyrethrin. $85 bi-monthly.`

### About + Team
Opening line: "[Owner first name] started [Company] in [year] because [specific founder moment]." Then 2-4 sentences on what the company stands for. Then name everyone on the team with one-line role.

Example: "Nelson Richardson started Richardson Pest in 2011 after his 2-year-old daughter was stung by an Arizona Bark Scorpion. We've been hunting them across the East Valley ever since."

### FAQ
Question-first, answer direct. Answer in 1-3 sentences. First sentence gives the answer, next sentences qualify.

Example:
> Q: Are your treatments pet-safe?
> A: Yes. We use pyrethrin-based products that are safe for dogs and cats once dry (about 30 minutes). We'll let you know when it's safe to let pets back in the treated area.

### Offers
Lead with the number. `[Offer amount or %] off [specific service] for [audience].`

Example: `$50 off your first service — new Mesa customers. Code: FIRSTTIME.`

### Service Areas (homepage section)
`We serve homeowners across [region]. Click your city for local details:` — then link each city to its page.

### CTA Band
Final push. One declarative sentence. Phone + primary CTA.

Example: `Same-day service. Saturday hours. One call.` → CTA: `Schedule a Visit` · phone `480-782-8111`.

### Footer
NAP: `[Business Name] · [Street Address] · [City, State ZIP] · [Phone] · Hours: [summary]` · License # · Social links.

## Industry-vocabulary preservation

From the Brand Brain Stage 7 fit signals, preserve the client's actual vocabulary. Never corporate-translate.

Preserve: "panel upgrade" (not "electrical service upgrade") · "rough-in" (not "pre-installation phase") · "tract communities" (not "residential developments") · "crawl space" (not "lower structural area") · "flat-rate" (not "fixed-fee billing").

## The Copy Checklist (apply before Stage 7)

Run through this before deploy. Missing any = the copy isn't shipping:

- [ ] Hero uses one of the 7 approved formulas
- [ ] Hero has exact-match keyword + city in H1
- [ ] Hero has phone number visible + large
- [ ] CTA button uses an approved phrase (not "Learn More" / "Click Here" / "Submit")
- [ ] Why You section is 3-5 BENEFITS (not features) with concrete numbers
- [ ] Before/After captions follow `[City]: [Problem]. [Solution].` format
- [ ] Reviews show first name + city + platform + star count
- [ ] Trust Numbers section has at least 3 stats with 2-4 word labels
- [ ] How It Works is 3-4 steps max, each 3-6 words
- [ ] Every service has one-line description with a concrete detail (price, timeline, method)
- [ ] About section leads with founder first name + year + specific founder moment
- [ ] FAQ answers are 1-3 sentences, direct-first
- [ ] Offers lead with number (`$50 off...` not `Great savings!`)
- [ ] Service Areas section links each city to its dedicated page
- [ ] CTA Band is one declarative sentence + primary CTA + phone
- [ ] Footer includes complete NAP + license # + hours + social
- [ ] No third-person language ("we" not "our company / the Richardson team")
- [ ] No filler adjectives ("quality" / "professional" / "reliable" without a number or proof attached)
- [ ] No corporate verbs ("utilize" → "use"; "ascertain" → "find out"; "commence" → "start")
- [ ] Voice register matches the preset per the table above
