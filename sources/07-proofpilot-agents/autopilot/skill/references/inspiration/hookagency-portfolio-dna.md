# Hook Agency — Portfolio Design DNA

## Why this doc exists

Hook Agency (Minneapolis, MN — founder Tim Brown) is a home-service marketing agency specialized in **roofing, plumbing, HVAC, and general contracting**, and they publish *real case studies with real metrics* rather than glossy tiles. Their client builds lean on **condensed/extended sans display faces (Uniform Bold, Knockout, Roboto Condensed, Libre 800, Oxanium, Noto Sans), brand-derived 3-to-4 color systems, shaped hero sections with angled/curved dividers and shaped photo masks, truck-side mascot motifs, and inline multi-step quote forms in the hero right rail**. When a ProofPilot demo is for a **roofer / plumber / HVAC tech / water heater specialist / exteriors+solar contractor / general contractor / restoration**, the target aesthetic is **Hook-Agency-built** — NOT newsroom serif editorial (no Fraunces, no Merriweather, no cream-and-sand). Hook leans harder into "home service professional" than either 180 Sites (shop-sign billboards) or Be The Anomaly (DFW pool/landscape luxe).

## Their own brand (hookagency.com)

- **Typography system:** **Champion** (Hoefler's extended compressed display) at **60px uppercase / 72px line-height / weight 500** for the hero H2 ("CHAMPIONING HOMETOWN HEROES"). **Museo Slab 800 uppercase at 13px / 1.3px letter-spacing** for tiny gray eyebrows ("HOME SERVICES MARKETING AGENCY"). **Museo Sans 400** for body at 16px in warm gray `rgb(70, 71, 71)`. Section H3s run Champion at 40px uppercase white-on-black. Three-face system — display / eyebrow-slab / body-sans — with very clear role separation.
- **Palette:** **Pure black `#000` dominant section bg**, pure white body bg, **acid-lime CTA `#D4FD52`** (rgb 212, 253, 82) — a high-saturation neon-yellow-green that reads as "construction PPE / fluorescent safety vest" energy. Warm gray body text `#464747`. Gray-300 `#ADB5BD` for eyebrows. No secondary accent.
- **Section rhythm signature:** Dark-video hero (technician loading a white van under a black gradient overlay) → "WE LOVE SERVING OUR INDUSTRY" 3-industry pill row (Roofing | HVAC | Plumbing) on black → "HOOK BETTER LEADS WITH A NICHE-SPECIALIZED TEAM" 3-up benefits → "TRUSTED HEROES JOIN US" logo strip → "SEE HOW EASY IT IS TO GET STARTED WITH US" 3-step process → testimonial band → **TRANSPARENT PRICING OPTIONS** 4-tier pricing grid ($3,000 / $7,300 / $11,200 / $16,400) — very unusual for an agency to publish pricing directly — → "YOU DESERVE A MARKETING AGENCY THAT CARES" → video embed → "TRUSTED BY THE INDUSTRY" → "THE #1 PLAYBOOK THAT MAKES YOU THE GO-TO IN TOWN" → FAQ accordion → "CHECK OUT OUR TOP RESOURCES" 3-card blog row → **"READY TO TAKE THE LEAP?" final CTA band on black with lime button** → footer.
- **Button discipline:** Primary = solid lime `#D4FD52` bg, **black text**, Museo Sans 600 at 15px, **4px radius** (tight), 12×24px padding, subtle 2px shadow `rgba(0,0,0,0.08) 0 2px 4px`. No uppercase. Labels: **"Schedule Intro Call," "Get Started Now," "Real Customer Experiences"** (with play-triangle icon). Secondary = underlined text link with inline play-triangle `▶`.
- **Motif approach:** (1) **Ink-splatter** decorative motif in the hero top-right corner — literal black ink splatter SVG as page decoration; also appears on the case-studies index and the final CTA. (2) **HOOK circle-hook logomark** — a circular outline with a hook glyph inside it, paired with the "HOOK AGENCY" wordmark. (3) **G-badge + Inc. 5000 + Best Biz Awards trust row** inside the hero — authority signals embedded above the fold. (4) **Authentic jobsite photography** — the hero is a real technician climbing into a work van, not stock.
- **Icon style:** **Lime-green line icons** (toggle-bars for "Proactive," horizontal-slider for "Cutting Edge," wifi-signal for "Transparent ROI Partner") at about 24×24 stroke — matches the Heroicons / Lucide stroke family.

**Vertical distribution:** **7 of 12 case studies (58%) are roofing / construction / restoration** — Hook's flagship vertical. **3 plumbing**, **3 HVAC** (with heavy overlap — several are combined plumbing+HVAC). **No pools, no landscaping, no locksmiths, no foam parties, no Christmas lighting.** Hook's niche is **trades with 4-figure-or-5-figure job tickets** (roof replacements, water heaters, full HVAC installs), not "service calls" trades. Contrast with 180 Sites (wide trades + mascot brands) and BtA (pools + outdoor living).

**Case-study writing signature:** Hook writes **500–800-word case studies with a 5-beat structure**: (1) intro paragraph about the client's pre-Hook frustration, (2) "THE RESULTS AT A GLANCE" bullet list with 3–4 real KPIs, (3) "Pain Points" bullet list, (4) a strategy narrative with H2s like "Cutting Through Competition with Paid Search" or "Rebuilding The Foundation For Performance," (5) "WHY THIS CASE STUDY MATTERS" close paragraph. They name revenue tiers ($2M starting rev), timeframes ("Since April 2024"), and refuse generic language. **The tiles themselves list a bold headline metric + 2 supporting stats + service tags — the exact component ProofPilot should emulate for any case-study grid.**

## Extracted DNA patterns (portable rules)

### Typography signature
**Extended and condensed sans in the display slot — never serifs, never scripts.** The Hook type library rotating across 3+ builds:

- **Hero display (H1/H2):**
  - **Champion** (Hoefler extended display) at 60–90px uppercase — Hook's own agency site.
  - **Knockout** (Hoefler condensed display) at 130px uppercase — Badgerland.
  - **Roboto Condensed 800** at 55px uppercase — Bears Plumbing. The "180-Sites-style shouting" variant.
  - **Uniform Bold 800** at 60px title-case — Genz-Ryan. The "friendly neighborhood" variant.
  - **Libre 800** (a modern high-contrast sans, not a serif despite the name) at 50px title-case — Young Construction. The "premium contractor" variant.
  - **Noto Sans 700** at 46px title-case — Monarch Roofing. The "softest" / most approachable variant.
  - **Oxanium 800** at 40px uppercase — Lorenz. The "techno-futurist 1970s-revival" variant.
  - **lft-etica** 600 italic uppercase at 36px — Speedy Water Heaters. The "mascot/racing" variant.

- **Eyebrow (tiny uppercase label above H1):** **Overpass 900 / Noto Sans 900 / Museo Slab 800 / AvenirPro 35 Light / Nunito Sans 900 / Proxima Nova 700** — all at **13–18px uppercase with letter-spacing**. Hook uses eyebrow tags on every build; this is **near-universal across the portfolio**.

- **Body:** Proxima Nova / Museo Sans / AvenirPro 55 / Nunito Sans / Overpass / Noto Sans / Uniform Reg — clean humanist/neo-grotesque sans at 14–16px weight 400. Never condensed.

**Weight discipline:** display 700–900. Eyebrow 800–900 uppercase (tiny caps). Body 400. Extreme weight contrast — same as 180 Sites.

**Case:** **~55% uppercase / ~45% title case** in hero H1/H2. Hook uses **title case more than 180 Sites does** (Young Construction, Monarch, Genz-Ryan are title-case), but **more uppercase than BtA does** (Speedy, Bears, Badgerland, Lorenz are uppercase). **Eyebrows are always uppercase.**

### Color discipline
- **4-color system max:** 1 dominant brand color + 1 accent (often CTA) + near-black (or pure black) + white. Occasionally a 3rd color when the client's heritage logo demands it (Genz-Ryan red+blue+yellow, Lorenz red+blue).

Lorenz red+blue (1970s script oval). Bears red+black (cartoon bear). Young Construction orange+navy (roof+asphalt). Speedy purple+yellow (mascot racing truck). Badgerland red+black (state-paw).

### Hook-specific: stated design philosophy + how it shows in execution
Hook's homepage positioning is **"Championing Hometown Heroes"** and **"We love serving our industry"** (specifically: Roofing, HVAC, Plumbing). The design philosophy is **niche-credibility-first**:

1. **"Niche-specialized team"** — Hook leads every case study with "the previous agency had 7 account managers in one year who didn't specialize in roofing" (Young Construction) or "needed a marketing partner who could implement the new brand across website and ads" (Speedy). The design output of this philosophy is: **every build looks like it belongs to that specific trade**, not a generic template. Bears looks like a plumber. Young Construction looks like a roofer. Monarch looks like a crown-branded roofer.

2. **Case-study prose-first, tile-second** — Hook writes real 500–800-word SEO-indexed case studies with metrics in the first paragraph and strategy narratives underneath. The tile on the case-studies index is the **headline metric + 2 supporting stats + WEB/SEO/PPC tags** — a data-first tile format ProofPilot should emulate directly.

3. **Design serves SEO/PPC** — Hook repeatedly explains design moves through an SEO lens: *"less code bloat, improved content structure, faster load times, all critical factors for modern SEO"* (Young Construction, Genz-Ryan). The visual rationale is rarely "this looks nice" — it's *"this loads faster, which ranks higher, which converts better."* ProofPilot's Designer Brain should inherit this framing.

4. **Transparent pricing** — Hook publishes **$3,000 / $7,300 / $11,200 / $16,400** monthly tiers directly on their homepage. Combined with real metrics in case studies, this creates a **"we're not hiding anything"** trust posture. ProofPilot demos could borrow this when positioning a local-service brand — publish pricing ranges on the homepage.

5. **Coupons + offers above-the-fold** — on HVAC/plumbing builds (Genz-Ryan, Bears), Hook puts a 3-4 offer grid directly below the hero. This is an e-commerce-inspired move adapted for home services — buyers scroll to the hero, then to the deals, then into service pages.

## Concrete copy-forward moves (8 actionable rules)

When Designer Brain is told "make it Hook Agency style" for a roofing/plumbing/HVAC demo, these moves are **required**:

1. **Pick one extended or condensed display sans at weight 700–900.** Default to **Roboto Condensed 800 at 55px uppercase** for plumbing/HVAC emergency-service brands, **Knockout 130px uppercase** for premium roofing/exteriors poster energy, **Noto Sans 700 at 46px title-case** for approachable "family-owned" home-service brands, **Libre 800 50px title-case** for premium-contractor brands, **Oxanium 800 40px uppercase** for techno-futurist heritage brands, **Uniform Bold 800 60px title-case** for mid-century-heritage brands like Genz-Ryan. **Never** Fraunces, Playfair, Merriweather, or any serif. Pair the display with **a tiny uppercase eyebrow in Overpass 900 / Nunito Sans 900 / Museo Slab 800 at 13–18px** — the eyebrow is Hook-universal.

2. **Pull the palette from the client's existing logo — do not invent a new brand.** If the client has a mascot (bear, water heater, crown, badger-paw), **amplify it at 1.5x scale in the hero** — put it in the logo, on the van photo, in section dividers, as a corner watermark. If the client has a heritage script wordmark (1950s red+blue oval, 1970s ribbon-script), **preserve it exactly** and use it as the nav logo. Hook's rule: **the client's existing brand is the source of truth. The website is the amplifier, not the reinvention.**

3. **Hero = split layout with headline + eyebrow + bullets + dual CTA on the left, real technician/van/jobsite photograph on the right, and an inline multi-step quote form floating in the right rail.** The quote form should be 3 steps: (1) ZIP code gate, (2) project type, (3) contact info. Background behind the form is a brand-color gradient. Add a **Google reviews pill with 3–4 overlapping avatar circles + "4.9 Star Google Rating / 100's of Reviews ★★★★★"** inside the hero below the bullets. This is Hook's universal rapid-conversion hero.

4. **Primary CTA = solid accent-color pill, white text, 700–800 weight, with a white circle containing a right-arrow icon on the right edge of the pill.** Radius: 8–10px for industrial/trades brands (Bears, Badgerland), 30–50px fully-rounded pill for approachable-service brands (Monarch, Genz-Ryan, Speedy). Labels: **"Book Appointment"** / **"Book Service"** / **"Schedule Now"** / **"Set Up Your Inspection"** / **"Get Started Now"** / **"Reach Out To Get Started"**. Pair with a secondary **solid near-black or ghost-outline** CTA for the phone number. **Never** use "Learn More" as the primary.

5. **Open the page with a dark promo bar ABOVE the main nav.** 32–40px tall, dark brand color, 1-line copy: **"Schedule Your Free Inspection Today!"** or **"Limited Time — $26 BOGO Furnace & A/C Tune-Up!"** or 3-city phone numbers side-by-side. This is a **Hook-signature move** that reads as "active business, fresh deals, call today."

6. **Immediately below the hero, add a 4-icon trust band — LOCAL / WORKMANSHIP / UPFRONT / ON-TIME (or vertical-specific variant).** Each icon in a red/brand-color circle, 60×60px, with an uppercase 2-line label (big word + small word underneath, e.g. "LOCAL / Family Owned"). This 4-icon strip is Hook's credibility-builder #1 and appears on most plumbing/HVAC builds directly below the hero.

7. **On plumbing/HVAC/roofing builds, include a coupon/offers grid directly below the trust band.** 3–4 coupon tiles with dashed borders + discount amounts ("$50 off Quote", "$26 Tune-Up", "Save $600 off Electrical Panel"). On roofing builds specifically, swap for a **"Financing That Works With Your Goals"** callout card with "Quick Approvals" badge. This is Hook's conversion-rate-protection move.

8. **Section rhythm target: 12–15 sections, alternating dark-brand-color bands with white/light-gray bands.** Sequence: Promo bar → Header → Hero → Trust band → Offers/Financing → Services grid → About/Why Us → Process → Reviews → Service area map → FAQ → Blog row → Final CTA → Footer. This is 2–4 sections longer than 180 Sites or BtA templates, and the length **is the point** — Hook pages are content-heavy to support SEO. Do not compress.

### Weaknesses / things NOT to copy
- **The "acid lime green CTA" from Hook's own site (`#D4FD52`) is a Hook-brand color, not a default.** Do not apply it to client builds — each Hook client has its own brand-derived accent. Use lime only if the client's logo has it.
- **The cartoon mascot is client-specific** — Bears has a bear because the name is Bears. Don't invent a mascot the client doesn't have; instead, preserve their existing logo shape.
- **130px Knockout hero type** (Badgerland) is the most design-forward move in the portfolio but **fails when the brand has a weak identity** — reserve it for premium-exteriors/solar brands that can sustain editorial-poster energy. Most plumbing/HVAC brands should use 40–60px headers instead.
- **Pure B&W grayscale photography** (Badgerland) is a deliberate monochrome move, not a default. Use it only when the brand is positioning around "premium / aesthetics-forward exteriors" — never on emergency-service plumbing/HVAC builds where real-color crew photos build trust.
- **Don't over-rotate letter-tricks.** Knockout's rotated "MADE" works once; using rotated letters on every build becomes gimmicky.
- **Hook's case-study pages have broken modal overlays and lazy-load stalls** — several case-study pages show console errors and slow hydration. Don't copy the technical performance — copy the prose structure + metric-led tile format.
