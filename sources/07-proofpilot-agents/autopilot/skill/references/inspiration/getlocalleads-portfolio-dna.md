# Get Local Leads — Portfolio Design DNA

## Why this doc exists

Get Local Leads (getlocalleads.agency, founder Jacob Jones) is a specialist agency building websites, SEO, and paid-ads stacks exclusively for **outdoor-living / hardscape / landscape / turf / paver / outdoor kitchen contractors** — a far narrower niche than 180 Sites (wide home-service trades), Be The Anomaly (DFW pool/landscape luxe), or Hook Agency (roofing/plumbing/HVAC). Their design output converges on a very specific stack: **title-case sans display with one Playfair-Display-italic accent word, dark-photo twilight hero, owner-or-team cutout on the right, Google-reviews pill inline, green accent, and a full-width accent-colored "Get A Free Quote" inline form band immediately below the hero.** When a ProofPilot demo is for a hardscape contractor, landscape design-build firm, paver installer, outdoor-kitchen builder, artificial-turf specialist, or "outdoor living" brand, the target aesthetic is **Get-Local-Leads-built** — *not* newsroom serif editorial, *not* 180 Sites' shouting Saira-900 billboard, *not* BtA's jewel-tone Bebas headlines, *not* Hook's red-van-truck roofer build.

## Their own brand (getlocalleads.agency)

- **Typography system:** **Inter, system-ui, sans-serif** is the only family — Jacob's own brand runs a single-typeface discipline similar to BtA's own purple-dot-grid site. H1 at **Inter 700, 96px, -2.88px letter-spacing**, title-case, dark on warm-white. H2 at **Inter 700, 48px**, white on dark. Body in Inter 400/16–20px. **Zero serifs, zero scripts, zero condensed display.** The signature hero move: "Websites" rendered as **outlined-stroke transparent text** against the page (webkit-text-stroke), while "Trust-Winning" is painted solid green — a 180-Sites-caliber "outline-stroked one word" technique but applied to the *whole word* ("Websites"), with the color highlight on the other word ("Trust-Winning"). Single-family + extreme weight contrast.
- **Palette:** Brand-green `#1E7B1E` (rgb 30, 123, 30) — a flat, not-too-bright kelly/forest green that reads as "landscape." Near-black/navy `#020617` (rgb 2, 6, 23 — Tailwind's `slate-950`). Warm-white body bg `#FAFAFA`. Mint-tinted soft-green circles `rgba(30, 123, 30, 0.08)-ish` as page motif. Three-color system: **green + near-black + warm-white**. No secondary accent, no yellow, no red.
- **Section rhythm signature:** Warm-white hero with outlined-stroke "Websites" headline + green-word highlight + dark-pill CTA + revenue/case-study social proof card → black "Trusted by Top Contractors" stats band (`$7M+ / 4.9★ / 100%`) → white "Selected Work / Websites that convert." portfolio carousel with prev/next arrows showing mockup screenshots → green marquee band ("WIN HOMEOWNER TRUST · DOMINATE YOUR MARKET · PREMIUM PRICING · INDUSTRY-LEADING RESULTS") scrolling horizontally → white featured case study row with metric cards (`$100K / 6 / 247%`) → 6-card case-study grid with star ratings and video thumbnails → "REAL RESULTS · The Proof Is in the Screenshots" dark proof-grid → "TESTIMONIALS · Loved by contractors" white review grid (8+ cards with "Read more") → black Problems vs Solutions split ("Tired of websites that don't convert?") → white 3-service split (Premium Website + Branding / Ongoing SEO / Lead Generation via Paid Ads) → "Which path is right for you?" decision matrix (4 buyer archetypes) → cream founder photo "Hello, I'm Jacob." personality section → white FAQ → green "Ready to Win Homeowner Trust?" final CTA band → black footer.
- **Button discipline:** Two variants, both **full 9999px pill**:
  - **Primary (home):** Solid green `#1E7B1E` bg, white text, **Inter 600 @ 18px**, **9999px radius (full pill)**, 16×32px padding, arrow-in-circle SVG on the right, no shadow.
  - **Nav "Book a Call":** Solid near-black `#020617` bg, white text, **Inter 600 @ 14px**, 9999px radius, 12×24px padding, external-arrow SVG inside a small white circle.
  - **Secondary:** Text-link with a play-triangle circle icon on the left ("▶ Watch Case Study"). No border, no bg — same pattern Hook uses on their own site.
- **Motif approach:** (1) **Green palm-tree keymark** paired with "GetLocalleads" wordmark in the nav — "local" = palm = Florida-coded (Jacob is Daytona Beach / FL). (2) **Soft green dotted circles** (large decorative blobs in the viewport corners) as background ornaments — reminiscent of BtA's purple dot-grid but used as soft watercolor shapes rather than a tile. (3) **Outlined-stroke hero word** motif. (4) **Sun-ray / fan-line decorative pattern** in the inline-form bands (most visible on the Pro Outdoor Living build — repeating fan-ray lines behind the yellow "Get A Free Quote" form), sometimes ported back to the GLL pages. (5) **Horizontal scrolling marquee** of value-props ("WIN HOMEOWNER TRUST · DOMINATE YOUR MARKET · PREMIUM PRICING") in alternating black and green bands — a distinctive repeating motif Jacob uses to reinforce the positioning.
- **Icon style:** **Lucide-stroke line icons** (green play-triangle, green checkmark, external-link arrow, sparkle) at ~20×20. No 3D, no gradient, no emoji, no filled icons. Google/Facebook/Trustpilot colorful logos embedded as real trust-badge SVGs (not fake stars).

## Extracted DNA patterns (portable rules)

### Typography signature

**Two-face discipline: one modern sans + one Playfair Display italic for the accent word.** The stable of display faces repeating across 3+ builds:

- **Modern sans for the body of the hero headline (600–700 weight, title-case, 50–74px):**
  - **InterDisplay 600** — Pro Outdoor Living at 62px. The "modern SaaS" variant.
  - **Uber Move 500/600** — Cinco Mosqueteros at 74px. The "tech-forward startup" variant.
  - **Doing 400/600** — KSJ Contracting at 60px. The "muscular graphic-tee" variant.
  - **Karla 700** — Saucedo at 54px with 4px letter-spacing. The "title-case editorial" variant.
  - **Space Grotesk 900** — Majha (current live) at 72px. The "modern geometric" variant (not GLL's mockup — Majha's live iteration).
  - **Inter 700** — GLL's own site at 96px. The "flat-neutral agency-default" variant.

- **Playfair Display italic as the 1-word accent inside the headline** — **this is the defining GLL typographic move.** Observed on:
  - **Pro Outdoor Living** — "Outdoor Living" in Playfair italic 600 at 62px inline with the sans headline.
  - **Turf Queen** (mockup) — "Artificial" in Playfair italic.
  - **Desert Pointe** (mockup) — "Masterpieces" in Playfair italic.
  - **NRX Landscaping** (mockup) — "Hardscaping & Landscape" in Playfair italic.
  - *At least 4 of 11 builds use this exact move.* Qualifies as a signature.

- **Body:** Inter, InterDisplay, Inter Tight, Poppins, Raleway-equivalents — humanist/neo-grotesque sans at 14–20px weight 400–500. Never condensed (exception: KSJ's Doing is condensed-adjacent).

**Weight discipline:** display at 500–700 (not 800–900 like 180 Sites/Hook). Body at 400–500. **Much lower weight-contrast than 180 Sites' Saira-900 vs. Roboto-400.** GLL headlines feel *quieter*, closer to BtA's Bebas-400 range but using modern humanist sans instead of condensed display.

**Case:** **~70% title-case, ~30% ALL CAPS in hero H1/H2.** This is the **most title-case-dominant portfolio of the four** — Pro Outdoor, Cinco, NRX-mockup, Turf Queen-mockup, Desert Pointe-mockup, Saucedo-live, Majha-live all title-case. Glen C and KSJ go ALL CAPS. Contrast: 180 Sites 70%+ CAPS, Hook ~55% CAPS, BtA ~50/50, **GLL ~70% title-case** — the softest, most editorial-friendly.

Electric-navy-blue (1 of 11) is a jewel-tone variant for premium-desert design-build. ### Section rhythm

Near-universal GLL template across the portfolio:

1. **Top-narrow-band** — dark or accent-color bar with phone + email + "Need any Landscaping & hardscaping help?" tagline + social icons. Similar to Hook's promo bar but *consistently* present (whereas Hook's is ~70% consistent).
2. **Header** — logo + nav + secondary phone pill + primary accent-color pill CTA ("Book Free Consultation" / "Book Your Free Consultation" / "Get A Free Quote").
3. **Dark photo hero** (twilight outdoor-living photo OR drone-aerial of completed job OR real-crew team portrait) with title-case H1 left-aligned, **Playfair italic accent word inside the headline** (on 4+ builds), short paragraph, dual CTA (solid pill + ghost pill), G-F or G-only reviews pill, sometimes a multi-platform trust badge row (Angi/Nextdoor/BBB), and on 5 of 11 builds — an **owner or team cutout photo** on the right side of the hero.
4. **Inline accent-color "Get A Free Quote" form band** — the single most-recurring GLL signature. A full-width accent-color horizontal bar with 3–5 form fields and a primary pill submit. Sits *immediately* below the hero, above-the-fold-adjacent. Pro Outdoor's version has a fan-sun-ray line pattern in the background. Cinco's is green. Desert Pointe's is blue. Most builds have exactly one of these bars.
5. **Services grid** — 4–6 photo cards with circular arrow-right icon badges, each leading to a service page.
6. **About / Why Us** — 3-up benefit with green/brand-color circle icons, or a split photo-and-copy block with "family-owned" or "30+ years" heritage framing.
7. **Gallery / Real Results** — 4–8 photo thumbnails of completed jobs (often before/after pairs).
8. **Reviews band** — Google reviews carousel or 3-up with G-logo + star row. **The G + F stacked-avatar pill** is the GLL signature in the hero; the reviews band also shows individual Google-avatar cards.
9. **Process** — numbered 3–5-step. Less visually consistent than BtA's timeline.
10. **Service area / map** — city list + embedded map card.
11. **FAQ accordion** — 5–8 questions, uppercase titles on KSJ/Glen C builds, title-case on Pro Outdoor / Cinco.
12. **Final CTA band** — accent-color or dark-photo, repeats the primary CTA.
13. **Footer** — 3-column with services, quick links, contact.

Typical GLL page length: **9–13 sections** — between BtA (10–11) and Hook (12–15). Shorter than Hook, comparable to BtA.

### Button discipline

- **Icon-in-circle pattern:** Several GLL buttons include a **right-arrow inside a white circle on the right edge of the pill** (GLL-own site "Book Discovery Call," KSJ "Book a Free Consultation" has a subtle arrow glyph). Same pattern as Hook's "white-circle-arrow" but less prominent.
- **Secondary:** (a) Ghost-outline pill (white text + white/accent border + transparent bg) — most builds have one. (b) Phone-number pill with phone-icon-in-white-circle on the left.
- **Top 6 canonical GLL CTAs:**
  - "Book Your Free Consultation" (Pro Outdoor, Turf Queen, Cinco)

### Motif approach

Less illustration-heavy than 180 Sites, less mascot-led than Hook, closer to BtA's "one real photograph anchors the brand" style — but with a distinctive GLL layer.

- **Primary motif: owner-or-team cutout on brand-color panel** — appears on 5+ builds (Glen C, Desert Pointe, Saucedo mockup, Cinco, Majha mockup). The owner is photographed in company polo/tee, cut out against a dark brand-color solid or gradient panel, arms-crossed or arms-spread "authority" pose. This is the **single most recurring GLL visual** and the closest analog to 180 Sites' "owner cutout on brand-color geometry" move.
- **Secondary motif: fan-sun-ray decorative line pattern** — behind the inline "Get A Free Quote" form on Pro Outdoor Living. A radiating line-burst SVG evoking desert sun/sunset. Doesn't repeat on every build but is the most distinctive purely-decorative motif in the portfolio.
- **Tertiary: green-chevron-zigzag dividers** — between every section on KSJ. A truck-wrap-stripe divider motif.
- **Word-level Playfair-italic highlight** — the typographic-motif version of BtA's block-highlighted-word. Painted differently (italic serif not solid color block) but the same underlying move: pick one noun in the H1 and treat it distinctly.
- **Multi-platform trust-badge row** — Google + Facebook + Angi + Nextdoor + BBB stacked in the hero (Desert Pointe, Glen C mockup). More aggressive than Hook's single-Google-pill or BtA's Google-pill-only.
- **Hand-drawn script accents** — "REQUEST YOUR FREE QUOTE TODAY" hand-marker green underline on KSJ. Turf Queen's full hand-drawn crown-script logomark. Craft-y artisan accents on 2 of 11 builds.
- **Palm-tree keymark (GLL own brand)** — a GLL-agency-signature, not a client-motif. Represents their "local" positioning (Jacob is Daytona Beach FL).

**GLL's motif rule:** When the client has a strong brand name (Cinco Mosqueteros → 5-musketeer silhouette logomark; Turf Queen → script-crown logomark) GLL amplifies it. When the client doesn't, GLL falls back to **owner-cutout + photo-hero + Playfair-italic-accent-word** as the universal recipe. There is **no portfolio equivalent of 180 Sites' Viking / Valkyrie / Professor mascot-world-building** — GLL does not invent archetypal mascot worlds; they use real-world human photography (owner + crew) as the brand anchor.

### Iconography

- **Service cards:** Green (or brand-accent-color) circle badges with **right-arrow** inside — a repeating "learn more" icon across the portfolio.
- **Checklist bullets:** Green-dot or green-checkmark in a circle (small, 16–24px).
- **Google + Facebook stacked-logos pill:** The **signature GLL reviews component** — real Google "G" + Facebook "f" + sometimes Trustpilot star icons stacked horizontally with a 5-star rating next to them, all inside a rounded pill. More composite than Hook's overlapping-avatars-pill.
- **Fan-sun-ray SVG** — decorative, not an icon, but a repeating vector motif on Pro Outdoor.
- **No 3D renders, no filled icons, no gradient icons, no emoji.** Same discipline as the other three agencies.

### Photography treatment

- **Hero:** Real photographs, often **twilight/blue-hour exteriors** of completed outdoor-living projects (outdoor kitchens, pool-side patios, fence-lined yards). Darkened 40–60% with a brand-color gradient or simply `rgba(0,0,0,0.4)` overlay so headline type reads. **Not stock.**
- **Owner/crew photos:** Cut out from a real photo, composited against a dark brand-color solid panel. The owner is almost always shown **face-front, arms-crossed, in a branded polo/tee**. Cinco's team photo shows 5 guys giving thumbs up in matching hoodies — the most "authentic crew" photo in the portfolio. Glen C's owner-in-sunhat is the folksiest.
- **Service/gallery photos:** Real job-site photos, heavy pool-side twilight and paver-patio glam shots. Higher-end photography on some builds (Pro Outdoor, NRX-mockup) than others (Majha, Saucedo). 
- **No duotone, no grayscale, no cream-filter editorial.** Photography is **realistic, saturated, twilight-warm** — closest parallel is BtA's drone-aerial twilight photography, but GLL favors lower angles (closer to patio-level) over high drone shots.
- **Mockups as portfolio deliverable:** **Every GLL portfolio tile is a laptop+iPhone 3D device-mockup rendering**, not a raw screenshot — so the portfolio reads "design studio reel" rather than "live-site index."

## Concrete copy-forward moves (8 actionable rules)

When Designer Brain is told "make it Get Local Leads style" for a hardscape / outdoor-living demo, these moves are **required**:

1. **Pick one modern humanist/grotesque sans for display at 500–700 weight (not 800/900).** Default to **InterDisplay 600 at 60–74px title-case** for general-purpose hardscape/outdoor-living brands. For tech-forward small crews, substitute **Uber Move 500/600** (Cinco route). For muscular-construction-tee energy, substitute **Doing 400/600 in ALL CAPS** (KSJ route). For boutique / feminine / women-owned turf & landscape firms, substitute a lighter Karla 700 or keep Inter 700 title-case. **Never Fraunces, Playfair-for-the-whole-headline, Saira-900, Montserrat-900, or any condensed-shouting display.** Pair the display with **body in Inter / Inter Tight / Poppins at 400–500 / 14–20px**.

2. **Pick one noun in the H1 and render it in Playfair Display italic 600 at the same point size as the sans headline.** This is the defining Get Local Leads signature and must be present on any GLL-styled demo. Examples: "Phoenix Premier *Outdoor Living* Design-Build Experts" / "Transforming Arizona Yards into Outdoor *Masterpieces*" / "Dallas-Fort Worth's *Artificial* Turf Experts" / "Toronto's Most Discerning *Hardscaping & Landscape* Design". The italic word is always the emotional/premium word (Masterpieces, Outdoor Living, Artificial, Hardscaping), never the functional word (Experts, Services, Company). One-and-only-one accent word per headline.

3. **Pick one brand accent color from the landscape-green + desert-warm-amber palette.** Default to **kelly-green `#10B981`** or **forest-green `#1E7B1E`** for most landscape/hardscape builds. Substitute **gold-orange `#FA7F0C`** for Phoenix/AZ outdoor-living builds. Substitute **electric-navy-blue** when the client's existing brand is navy. Pair with **tinted near-black (`#020617`, `#0A1628`, `#0F1D2A`)** and **warm-white (`#FAFAFA`, `#FFFBF6`)**. 3-color system. Never red/yellow/purple as primary; these read wrong for the outdoor-living buyer.

4. **Hero = dark twilight photo of a completed outdoor-living job with an owner or team cutout composited on the right side.** Use an actual client-built patio / outdoor kitchen / paver driveway photo shot at blue-hour (not stock). Darken the bg 40–60% with `rgba(0,0,0,0.4)` gradient. Place title-case H1 (with Playfair-italic accent) + 2-sentence body + **dual CTA pill (solid accent + ghost)** + **G + F stacked-logos pill with 5-star rating** on the left. Place owner-in-company-polo cutout OR team-portrait cutout on the right. If the client can't supply real owner photos, fall back to a single drone-aerial hero (BtA-style) — but the cutout-on-the-right is preferred.

5. **Primary CTA = solid accent-color pill with 9999px or 50px radius, white text, modern-sans 500–700 weight, title-case ("Book Your Free Consultation" / "Book a Free Consultation" / "Get A Free Quote").** No uppercase, no shadow (except in the KSJ Doing/radial-gradient variant where `4px 4px 0 0` offset shadow is the signature). Include a **right-arrow inside a white circle on the right edge** of the pill on ~50% of builds. Pair with ghost-outline secondary CTA and/or phone-icon-in-white-circle pill for the phone number. Primary CTA label is *always* "Book Free Consultation" or "Get A Free Quote" — these are the 2 canonical GLL CTAs.

6. **Directly below the hero, put a full-width accent-color inline "Get A Free Quote" form band with 3–5 horizontal fields and a solid pill submit.** Background color matches the primary accent but lighter (yellow-cream for gold-orange builds, light-green for green builds, light-blue for blue builds). For Phoenix/AZ builds, add a **fan-sun-ray line-pattern SVG** behind the form (Pro Outdoor signature). This band should sit immediately after the hero photo, above-the-fold-adjacent on desktop. This is the single highest-conversion GLL signature — skipping it breaks the template.

7. **Reviews proof lives inside the hero as a G + F stacked-logos pill.** Use the real Google "G" and Facebook "f" logos stacked horizontally (overlap by 20%) next to "5.0 ★★★★★ (X reviews)" in a small rounded pill card. Include Trustpilot star or Angi logo if the client has them. For premium desert-outdoor-living builds (Desert Pointe), stack a full trust-badge row (Facebook + Google + Angi + Nextdoor + BBB) inside the hero. Never use fake-star spam — always real logo SVGs.

8. **Section rhythm template:** Top narrow promo bar → Header → Dark photo hero with owner/team cutout → Inline accent-color "Get A Free Quote" form band → Services grid with circle-arrow card badges → About / Why-Us 3-up → Real Results gallery → Reviews carousel with G-avatar cards → Process 3–5-step → Service area + city list → FAQ accordion → Final accent-color CTA band → Dark footer. Target **10–13 sections** — **shorter than Hook's 12–15, longer than 180 Sites' 8–10**. Alternate dark-photo sections with white / accent-color bands roughly every other section.

### Weaknesses and things NOT to copy

- **The portfolio presentation (laptop+iPhone device-mockup renders) is a weakness if the goal is "proof of live launches"** — 2 of 11 tiles link out to a live site, and several mockups represent unlaunched concepts (Verdant Edge Unlimited's domain didn't resolve at recon; Stone Aura has no discoverable live site). If a ProofPilot demo needs to *prove* live-URL outcomes, Hook Agency's real-case-study format with metrics and a named live URL is the stronger presentation. Do not copy GLL's deliverable-mockup-only approach for performance-case-study work.
- **Some client sites' GLL mockups look substantially different from the current live site** — e.g., Majha's mockup shows an orange-panel dark-hero redesign but the live site runs a Space Grotesk 900 dark variant; Glen C's mockup shows a dark-navy/green redesign but the live runs an older cream-and-green theme. This means the GLL portfolio is more "proposed design" than "shipped design" for many entries. When referencing GLL for inspiration, **verify the mockup matches a live build before treating it as a proven pattern** (use Pro Outdoor, Cinco, KSJ as the 3 confirmed-live anchors).
- **The Playfair-italic single accent word is powerful but easily over-used** — if every GLL-styled demo has a Playfair italic word in the same position, the trick wears out. Vary: sometimes the accent can be **color-painted** (BtA-style), sometimes **outline-stroked** (180-Sites-style) — don't commit Playfair to every single headline.
- **The fan-sun-ray pattern is Pro-Outdoor-specific** — seen in one build. Use it for Phoenix / AZ desert clients specifically, not as a default GLL motif.
- **The 11-project portfolio is thin** — compared to 180 Sites (56), BtA (79), or Hook (12 but with deep case-study prose each), GLL's book reads **new-agency small**. Don't over-index on "GLL style is a signature" when it's a small sample. Treat it as an emerging lane rather than a mature playbook.
- **"Uber Move" font (Cinco) is licensed proprietary** — don't ship it on a ProofPilot demo. Substitute **Inter Tight 700** or **Manrope 700** for a similar humanist-grotesque feel.
- **KSJ's radial-gradient CTA button with chunky offset shadow** is the one clearly-BtA-copied move in the portfolio — use it *only* when the client demands a muscular-sticker feel over the softer GLL default.
