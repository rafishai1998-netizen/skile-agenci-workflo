# 180 Sites — Portfolio Design DNA

## Why this doc exists

180 Sites is a Nashville-based web agency that builds exclusively for home-service and trades brands. Their output is the opposite of what ProofPilot's default Designer Brain (Gemini 3.1 Pro) produces: **no serifs, no newsroom energy, no tasteful tan-and-cream** — instead, bold geometric or condensed sans at extreme weights, truck-side color blocking, committed per-brand motifs, and hero headlines that read like a shop sign from 30 feet away. When a future ProofPilot demo is for an electrician, plumber, HVAC, roofer, pressure washer, pest control, Christmas-lighting, concrete, or junk-removal brand, the target aesthetic is **180-Sites-built**, not "Fraunces magazine editorial."

## Their own brand (180sites.com)

- **Typography system:** Quicksand 600–700 for the primary display (rounded geometric sans, friendly-but-stable) paired with Nunito 400/600 as the humanist body/H2 companion. Both are soft-edge rounded geos — not the aggressive blackletter energy of their clients. 180 Sites keeps their *own* brand soft because their clients get the loud voice.
- **Palette:** Deep navy text `#151D43`, pure white bg, signature yellow CTA `#FFC300`, a secondary cyan-blue accent. Zero cream, zero sand, zero warm beige.
- **Section rhythm signature:** Hero (headline + dual CTA + illustrated hero graphic) → portfolio grid dominates the page → 180 Template vs Custom split → CTA band → testimonials → money-back guarantee reassurance → footer.
- **Button discipline:** Primary = solid yellow `#FFC300` bg, navy text, 5px radius, Quicksand 700 at 18–22px, no shadow. Secondary = white bg with navy outline, same dimensions.
- **Motif approach:** A minimal "180" keymark logo (stylized 0 as a circular arrow) + large bubble-accent circles (cyan, yellow, navy stacked) behind the hero photograph. Restrained.
- **Icon style:** Lucide-style thin stroke checkmark icons, waving-hand emoji for the guarantee line. Zero custom illustration in the chrome.

## Extracted DNA patterns (the portable rules)

### Typography signature
**Geometric and condensed sans exclusively. Zero serifs, zero script, zero humanist body type pretending to be editorial.** The stable of faces recurring across 3+ builds:

- **Display headers:** Saira, Montserrat, Poppins, Teko, Orbitron, Grandstander (for playful brands like Big Foam). All used at **800–900 weight**, 60–120px above-fold.
- **Eyebrow / secondary display:** Chakra Petch, Barlow Condensed, Saira at 600–700.
- **Body:** Roboto, Barlow, Open Sans, Inter Tight, Manrope, Lato, DM Sans — boring, geometric, highly legible sans at 400.

**Weight discipline:** extreme contrast. Display is almost never lighter than 700, almost always 800 or 900. Body is 400, occasionally 500. Middle weights (500–600) are used only for eyebrows and H3 service-card titles.

**Case:** Display headlines are **uppercase** on ~70% of builds. When not uppercase (Professor Pressure, 180 Sites' own site), they're title case. Never sentence case for H1/H2.

### Color discipline
- **One dominant brand color + one CTA color + white + near-black navy**, nothing more. 4-color system max.
- Primary is pulled from the client's logo or industry convention (blue for water/cleaning, purple for "legendary" brands, red for Christmas, gold for tactical trades).
- CTA color is almost always **high-saturation** — orange `#FF9143`, yellow `#FFCD00`, red `#F10800`, electric blue `#2788FC`. Never muted, never dusty.
- Near-black is never `#000` — always a tinted navy like `#0A1838`, `#090D22`, `#151C24`. This is a 180-Sites signature (and Be-The-Anomaly signature too).
- Backgrounds alternate navy sections with white/off-white sections to chunk the page into scannable bands. No sand/cream editorial backgrounds.

### Section rhythm
Near-universal template across the portfolio:

1. **Hero** — full-bleed photo or illustration, headline in massive display weight (often stacked 3 lines uppercase), 1–2 CTAs, sometimes inline quote form in right rail.
2. **Trust-band** — either a logo strip ("As Seen On," BBB, Angi, HomeAdvisor) or a 3–4 icon benefit row.
3. **Services grid** — 4, 6, or 8-card grid with photo or icon per card, usually on dark navy with hover-reveal arrows.
4. **Why choose us / About** — 3-up benefit or owner-story section.
5. **Guarantee band** — bright accent-color solid band ("100% Satisfaction Guaranteed" callout).
6. **Social proof** — review cards + service-area map.
7. **3-step process** — numbered cards ("Get a Fast Quote → Set a Schedule → Sit Back & Relax").
8. **FAQ accordion** — 5–8 questions, near-identical accordion component across builds.
9. **Final CTA band** — repeats the hero CTA with urgency framing ("Ready for legendary electrical service?").
10. **Footer** — service areas list, contact columns, social icons.

### Button discipline
- **Primary:** Solid accent-color bg, high-contrast dark or white text, **bold 700–800 weight**, **UPPERCASE**, radius ranges from 0px (industrial — Haro, TAGG) → 10px (default — most builds) → 50px pill (Professor Pressure only). An icon (lightning, chevron, phone) is common *inside* the button, not separate.
- **Secondary:** Either outlined version of primary, or reversed-color pill (phone number as inverse of primary). Always paired, never solo.
- CTAs read like instructions, never clever: **"Get A Fast Quote," "Get Pricing," "Click To Call," "Book Now"**. "Get A Fast Quote" appears on ~8 of the builds I deep-scanned.

### Motif approach
**Every client gets one committed motif, and that motif shows up in 3+ places** (logo, hero background, section dividers, quote-mark icon). Not "a nice illustration on page 1" — a world.

Observed examples:
- Volt Vikings → Norse/Viking mountain scene
- Professor Pressure → bearded professor + bubbles + curved water waves
- TAGG → diagonal concrete brush slashes + hexagon
- Haro → shield + gold lightning slashes
- Valkyrie → winged warrior + diagonal stripes
- Patriot Illumination → patriotic shield + greenery
- Big Foam → cartoon dinosaur mascots + bubble cloud type

**This is the single most important signature to copy.** The motif is usually derived from the brand name (Vikings → actual Viking, Valkyrie → actual Valkyrie, Professor → actual professor), and 180 Sites will push the client toward a name/brand that *enables* a motif. Generic "ABC Electric" gets harder to brand in this system.

### Iconography
- **Service cards:** Thin-stroke custom or lucide-ish line icons, usually brand-color or white on dark bg. Never flat filled icons.
- **Trust/process bullets:** Font Awesome solid checkmarks in brand-accent color.
- **CTA accents:** Lightning bolt, chevron arrow, phone receiver — embedded inline in headlines and buttons.
- **Almost never:** Noto Emoji, 3D illustrations, stock icon bundles, heavy gradient icons.

### Photography treatment
- **Real photos of the owner/team** on about-us sections, with the subject *cut out* from background and composited against brand-color geometry (Haro, Volt Vikings team shots, Valkyrie). This is a signature move.
- **Hero photos** are either darkened with a color-tint overlay matching the brand (Professor Pressure blue, TAGG navy) or replaced entirely with brand illustration (Volt Vikings).
- **Service/portfolio photos** are always real-job work, not stock.
- **No duotone editorial effects.** No washed-out cream filters. No moody editorial greys.

## When to invoke "180-Sites style" for a ProofPilot demo

**Use when the client is:**
- Electrician, plumber, HVAC tech, roofer, pressure washer, window cleaner, pest control, tree service, concrete, garage door, exterior cleaning, outdoor/Christmas lighting, junk removal, landscaping, handyman, carpet cleaning, mobile detailing, vehicle wraps, foam parties, or any similar trade.
- Named something brandable (Volt Vikings-tier — a noun + noun / an archetype + trade pattern). If the name is a strong archetype (Viking, Knight, Pirate, Patriot, Professor, Samurai), lean harder into motif.
- Targeting residential or small-commercial buyers who respond to shop-sign energy and "legendary service" copy, not dentists/lawyers/financial advisors.

**Don't use when:**
- The client is a dentist, lawyer, financial advisor, therapist, wedding venue, luxury interior designer, high-end architect, museum, or any brand whose buyers want quiet/editorial confidence. 180 Sites' portfolio actually has a dentistry build (Svoma) — it's notably the quietest and probably their weakest brand fit.
- The client name is generic and has no archetype to build a motif from ("City Services LLC"). In that case Hook Agency's cleaner template-driven aesthetic is better than trying to force 180 Sites' motif energy.
- The brand already has an editorial/hospitality aesthetic (boutique hotel, premium food brand, fashion label).

## Concrete copy-forward moves

When Designer Brain is told "make it 180-Sites style" for a home-service demo, these moves are **required**:

1. **Pick one geometric-sans display face at weight 800 or 900.** Default to Montserrat 900 for general-purpose, Saira 900 for electrical/tech, Teko 900 for roofing/contractor, Poppins 800 for cleaning/washing, Orbitron 900 for futurist/wraps/EV. Never Fraunces, Merriweather, Playfair, or any serif.

2. **Stack the hero headline three lines in ALL CAPS at 80–120px.** Example: "LEGENDARY / ELECTRICAL / SERVICES" (Volt Vikings) or "CHRISTMAS / LIGHTING / SERVICES" (Patriot). The headline must be readable from across the parking lot.

3. **Use tinted near-black as your dark color, never `#000`.** Pull a navy in the `#0A–0F` lightness / 20–30° blue hue range (`#0A1838`, `#151C24`, `#090D22`). Pair with a high-saturation single accent (orange, yellow, red, electric blue) and white.

4. **Commit to one motif tied to the brand name.** If the client is "Volt Vikings" — put a Viking in the logo, a Viking mountain in the hero, and runes/zigzags in the dividers. Derive at least 3 repeating motif usages. If the name doesn't enable a motif, pick a shape-language motif (TAGG's diagonal slashes, Valkyrie's angular stripes).

5. **Primary CTA = solid accent bg, dark text, 700–800 weight uppercase, icon inline, 8–10px radius.** Label it "Get A Fast Quote" or "Get Pricing." Always pair with a secondary phone-number CTA in inverse treatment.

6. **Section rhythm template:** Hero → Trust bar → Services grid (dark bg, 4–6 cards) → Why Us → Guarantee band (accent color) → Reviews → 3-step Process → FAQ → Final CTA → Footer. Never more than 10 sections. Never fewer than 8.

7. **Alternate dark navy and white sections.** Every other section flips background color. This creates scannable chunks — one of the clearest rhythmic signatures in the portfolio.

8. **Real owner/team photo with cut-out + brand-color geometric backdrop.** If the client supplies a photo of the owner (or technician), cut them out from their background and place them over a diagonal-slash or geometric brand-color panel. This is the single most recognizable 180-Sites photo treatment.

### Weaknesses to avoid copying
- **Service-card grids can feel samey** — the same 4-card-on-dark pattern repeats across dozens of builds. When possible, differentiate one card size (feature card) or break the grid with a photo.
- **Overuse of "GET A FAST QUOTE" CTA label.** Cute, but if every home service site in the portfolio says the exact same thing, it's a tell. Vary at least the secondary CTA.
- **Some builds lean too hard on stock photography behind the hero** — the ones that work best (Volt Vikings, Valkyrie, Big Foam) use brand-world illustration instead. When the demo has no budget for custom illustration, lean into the motif via SVG shapes rather than generic stock.
- **The dentistry and senior-living builds in the portfolio (Svoma, Creating New Hope) are visibly weaker** — the loud-trades aesthetic doesn't transfer. Don't force it on the wrong vertical.
