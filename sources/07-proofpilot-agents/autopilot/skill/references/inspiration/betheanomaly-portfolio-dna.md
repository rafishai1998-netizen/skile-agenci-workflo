# Be The Anomaly — Portfolio Design DNA

## Why this doc exists

Be The Anomaly (Anomaly Marketing Co., Dallas/Fort Worth) is a "founded by tradesmen, for tradesmen" agency whose own brand runs on **Bebas Neue display + Work Sans body + a single purple accent**, and whose client builds lean heavily on **chunky condensed/geometric sans** (Bebas Neue, Montserrat 800, Russo One, Titillium Web), alternating **near-black navy and white section bands**, **drop-shadowed buttons with 6–8px radius**, and **one high-saturation brand accent per build**. When a ProofPilot demo is for a pool company, locksmith, tree service, concrete contractor, landscaper, outdoor-living builder, or similar DFW-style blue-collar trade, the target aesthetic is **Be-The-Anomaly-built** — NOT newsroom-serif editorial (no Fraunces, no Merriweather, no cream-and-sand).

## Their own brand (betheanomaly.io)

- **Typography system:** **Bebas Neue 400** is the display face everywhere — all H1/H2/H3/nav labels. Condensed, uppercase-by-default sans at a single weight. **Work Sans 400/500/600/700** is the full body companion. A third face, **Roboto Condensed 300–700**, is loaded but used sparingly for small labels. Total system is 2 face families with Bebas as the hero. H1 = Bebas Neue 400 @ 70px / 78px line-height / +1px letter-spacing, white.
- **Palette:** Single brand accent **purple `#7A54CB`** (rgb 122, 84, 203). Near-black text `#1A1B1F`. Pure white. Off-white/bone body background `#F5F2EB`-ish with a **purple dot-grid SVG tile** layered over it. No secondary colors, no gradients.
- **Section rhythm signature:** Dark video/photo hero (drone aerial of residential home) → stats + intro → carousel of phone mock-ups showing client work → "Not Your Regular Marketing Agency" intro → **tabbed portfolio** (Blue Collar | Other Industries) with 6-card grids and pagination → 6-step numbered process timeline ("kick-off → Pillar 1 Social Proof → Pillar 2 CRO Website → Pillar 3 Walking Billboards → Paid Advertising → SEO") → benefits 3-up → stats counter → inline Calendly → footer. Hero is the only dark section — the rest is bone/white with purple accents.
- **Button discipline:** Two variants.
  - **Pill CTA (in-copy):** Purple `#7A54CB` solid, white text, **30px radius** (full pill), Work Sans 700 16px/20px line-height, 12×24px padding, white arrow SVG inline. Not uppercase.
  - **Nav CTA "Book A Call":** Purple bg, 8px radius, **1px white border**, **signature offset shadow `box-shadow: 0 5px 0 0 #fff`** — the "chunky floating button" tell. This offset-shadow move repeats on client builds with a black or navy shadow.
- **Motif approach:** (1) "Anomaly" logo uses a 4×5 dot-matrix grid as a mark next to the wordmark — the dots become a recurring motif. (2) Purple **underline squiggle** highlights the key noun in headlines (e.g., under "Blue Collar Business"). (3) Purple **capsule tag** behind eyebrow labels ("Our Portfolio"). (4) Purple **angled-cut ribbon** across the top bar — a truck-wrap stripe motif.
- **Icon style:** Purple-filled circles `#7A54CB` containing white Heroicons/Tabler-style stroke icons at 36×36. Checkmark bullets use the same purple circle treatment. No 3D, no gradients, no illustrations in the chrome.

### Build 1: Anomaly Pool Services (Pool Cleaning — DFW) — *the canonical BtA template*

- **URL:** https://www.anomalypoolservices.io/
- **Screenshots:** `build-3-anomalypool-hero.png`, `build-3-anomalypool-fullpage.png`
- **Why first:** This is the BtA founder's own pool company — it is the reference implementation of the template.
- **Typography stack:** `"Bebas Neue", sans-serif` 400 for all display. H1/H2 at **80px / 78px line-height**, title-case (mixed case, not all caps) — uncommon for condensed display type and a clear BtA signature. `Work Sans 400/500/600/700` as body (20px / 28px). 400 italic variant used for a hand-drawn hype line "People Can't Stop Raving!" in cyan.
- **Palette:** Cyan/sky blue primary `#4CA8DE` (rgb 76, 168, 222). Near-black text `#121A1E` (rgb 18, 26, 30). Pure white body bg. Hero headline uses **alternating black + cyan coloring word-by-word**: "WE MAKE **DFW POOL CLEANING** STRESS-FREE BY PROVIDING AN ANOMALY EXPERIENCE" — "DFW POOL CLEANING" is cyan, the rest black.
- **Section rhythm:** Hero (headline + dual CTA + cyan "People Can't Stop Raving!" squiggle → team photo carousel with blue step-shape underlay) → Free 21-point inspection quote form → **Dark section** "Discover the Anomaly Difference" → services grid on white with cyan badges → **5-step process timeline** (numbered navy dots with alternating L/R image cards) → THIS IS THE ANOMALY DIFFERENCE three-up → reviews trio → MEET THE AMAZING TEAM portrait row → FAQ accordion → service-areas map → CTA/footer.
- **Button primary:** Solid cyan `#4CA8DE`, white text, Work Sans 700 @ 16px, **8px border radius**, 12×24px padding, **`border: 1px solid #121A1E`**, **`box-shadow: 0 5px 0 0 #121A1E`** (signature offset chunky black shadow — the "truck bumper sticker" button). Inverse pairs on dark sections have a white shadow. Label = "Get a Free Quote" or "Get Quote".
- **Committed motif:** The **Anomaly dot-grid tile** (4×5 small circles) appears next to every logo lockup and repeats as a micro-pattern. Hand-drawn curly arrow + italic "People Can't Stop Raving!" badge as a hype embellishment. **Exit-intent pop-up** offering "YOUR FIRST FILTER CLEAN IS ON US" with a live 60-second countdown and Take Quiz CTA — aggressive lead-gen.
- **Distinctive moves:** (1) One cyan word inside the black headline = BtA's preferred word-level color-highlight technique (vs. 180 Sites' "stroke-outline one word" or Redrock's "box-highlight one word"). (2) Italic hand-drawn squiggle copy next to reviews. (3) Interactive 15-second quiz gating the hero quote form. (4) The quote form itself is gamified with a ticking timer.

- **Button primary:** Solid green `#42AC54`, white text, **Serif 72 Beta Italic 700 @ 20px**, **0px border radius** (square corners — editorial), 16×32px padding. Secondary = 0-radius white ghost with thin white border. Labels: "Schedule Discovery Call," "Learn More."
- **Committed motif:** Minimal editorial restraint — no shape motif at all. The commitment is entirely typographic (italic serif everywhere) and photographic (sun-dappled Texas-estate photography). The green-only-for-CTA discipline and the cream background do the brand work.
- **Distinctive moves:** (1) Serif body + italic display in a trades portfolio = BtA intentionally signaling "we can also do luxury." (2) 0-radius square buttons (vs. 6–30px on every other build) — the square edges read "architectural" and "expensive." (3) No stats band, no badge strip, no motif decoration — instead the rhythm is editorial prose + photo + editorial prose. (4) **This is the template to emulate when a ProofPilot client is explicitly high-end design-build ($50k+ project size) and wants to feel like a boutique firm, not a shop.**

## Extracted DNA patterns (portable rules)

### Typography signature
- **Two dominant display routes:**
  - **(A) "Bebas Neue title-case" route** — Bebas Neue 400 at 70–80px in title case (not all caps), black + one accent color for word-level highlighting. Used on Anomaly Pool, Anomaly Marketing's own site, several pool/cleaning builds. This is *different* from 180 Sites' "Montserrat 900 ALL CAPS" shouting style — BtA's hero typography is **calmer, longer-reading, and not as aggressive**.
  - **(B) "Geometric-sans 800 uppercase" route** — Montserrat 800, Russo One 400, or Titillium Web 700 at 72–80px, often uppercase. Used on PowerKeys, Redrock, Salas. Closer to 180 Sites aesthetic but still typically title-cased (Redrock).
- **Body is always a humanist or neo-grotesque sans:** Work Sans, Nunito Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, Raleway, Helvetica Neue. Always at 16–20px, 400–600 weight. Never condensed.
- **Signature divergences:** Kingswood (Serif 72 Italic) is the luxury-serif outlier. Salas (Titillium Web outlined hero + script logo) is the craft-artisan outlier. 90% of the portfolio stays in routes A and B.
- **Weight contrast:** 800/900 displays paired with 400–600 body. Same extreme contrast as 180 Sites.
- **Case:** About **50/50 title-case vs. ALL CAPS** — notably less uppercase-dominant than 180 Sites (70%+ uppercase). BtA gives headlines room to breathe.

### Color discipline
- **3-color system:** 1 high-saturation brand accent + 1 near-black (tinted navy or charcoal, never `#000` in hero sections) + white or warm-off-white.
- **Observed accents across verticals:**
  - Pools → cyan blue `#4CA8DE` (Anomaly Pool) or teal `#0085AD` (CBD Concrete)
  - Locksmith → green `#46C342` (PowerKeys)
  - Outdoor living → crimson red `#A91D3A` (Redrock)
  - Tree/landscape → forest green + gold `#0A660E / #E0A423` (Salas)
  - Luxury landscape → hunter green `#42AC54` (Kingswood)
  - Agency-own → purple `#7A54CB` (BtA itself)
- **Red-crimson, cyan, and green are the three most common accents** — BtA picks the saturation a half-shade less pure than 180 Sites (compare `#A91D3A` BtA-red to `#F10800` 180-Sites-red; BtA leans toward *jewel tones*, 180 Sites leans toward *candy tones*).
- **Near-black is always tinted:** `#121A1E`, `#16181D`, `#1A1B1F`, `#061939`. Consistent with 180 Sites discipline.
- **Alternating section bands:** Every build flips between dark-accent sections and white/off-white sections roughly 50/50 down the page. This rhythm is near-universal (Anomaly Pool has 6 light and 5 dark sections in its 11-section rhythm; Redrock, PowerKeys, CBD all follow).

### Section rhythm
Near-universal template across the portfolio (minor reordering between builds):
1. **Hero** — full-bleed photo (often drone-aerial of a completed job, not the usual "tech in front of white van"), H1 in display sans at 70–80px with **one word highlighted** (cyan/red/gold) either via color, underline, or rectangular block, dual CTA (primary solid + ghost), Google 5-star pill above or below the H1.
2. **Stats band** — 4 metrics (X+ Projects, X+ Years, X+ Customers, 100% Satisfaction) on a dark or accent bg.
3. **Services grid** — 4–6 photo cards, each with a photo, title, micro-copy, and a dual CTA (solid "Book Now" + text "Learn More"). Usually on dark bg.
4. **About / Why Us** — horizontal split with photo + owner copy, often emphasizing "family-owned" or "founded by tradesmen."
5. **Benefits 3–4 up** — icon-led benefit cards on alternating dark or light bg.
6. **Process** — numbered 3-to-5-step list. Often a vertical timeline with accent-color dots.
7. **Reviews band** — 3–4 Google-review cards with the actual Google "G" badge (not fake stars).
8. **Service-area map + city list** — sometimes as one section, sometimes split.
9. **Inline contact form** — accent-color section with quote form embedded directly, not behind a modal.
10. **FAQ accordion** — 5–8 questions.
11. **Footer** — menu + contact + social icons.

**Key contrast with 180 Sites:** BtA reviews come in much earlier (often right after services, sometimes even in the hero via a Google pill) — the *trust proof leads the narrative* more than on 180 Sites builds, where reviews often appear late. BtA also runs **inline embedded forms** more frequently than modal pop-ups (except Anomaly Pool's exit-intent).

### Button discipline
- **Primary:** Solid accent-color bg, white or dark text, **weight 700–800**, **6–8px radius default** (rarely 0px — Kingswood; rarely 13px — Salas; once 30px pill — BtA's own site). 12×24–32px padding. Usually a **chunky offset shadow `0 5px 0 0 [dark]`** for floating-chip effect (Anomaly Pool, BtA-own site nav); some builds omit shadow for cleaner look (Redrock, PowerKeys, CBD).
- **Secondary:** Ghost/outline of primary (1px border, transparent bg, white or accent text), identical dimensions.
- **Labels — the top 5 canonical BtA CTAs:**
  - "Get a Free Quote" (Anomaly Pool, CBD Concrete)
  - "Request A Free Estimate" / "Free Estimate" (Redrock, Salas)
  - "Get Free Estimate" (PowerKeys)
  - "Book Now" (PowerKeys, most service cards)
  - "Book A Free Growth Call" (BtA's own CTA — their unique variant)
- Secondary CTAs are almost always either "Contact Us" or "Learn More" — extremely consistent.

### Motif approach
Less committed than 180 Sites. **Most BtA builds do NOT have an archetypal character/mascot** — they rely on:
- **Hero word-level color highlight** (BtA's unique signature — one noun in the H1 gets painted the accent color, underlined, or block-highlighted). Compare to 180 Sites' outline-stroked word.
- **Dot-grid tile** on the Anomaly Marketing wordmark — reappears only on BtA's own pages and Anomaly Pool.
- **Chiseled logomark** — geometric key/leaf/rock icons, usually 1-color.
- **One real photo moment that anchors the brand** — Shaq for PowerKeys, the drone-aerial for CBD, the tree-canopy for Salas, the estate twilight for Redrock. BtA invests in one **hero photograph** rather than a world-building illustration system.

The strongest mascot/character example in the portfolio is **Shaq for PowerKeys** — an actual endorsement rather than a drawn mascot. This is a tell for BtA's go-to-market: they will secure or fabricate a celebrity/authority-figure moment over commissioning custom illustration. Budget-wise that's a different bet than 180 Sites' Viking/Valkyrie illustrator route.

### Iconography
- **Service cards:** Thin-stroke white icons in accent-color circles (PowerKeys green, Anomaly Pool cyan, BtA purple). Closer to Heroicons/Tabler than Lucide — slightly chunkier stroke.
- **Checklist bullets:** Accent-color circles with white checkmark (BtA's 36×36 purple circle + white check is the template).
- **Google review badges:** Real Google "G" logo SVG embedded inline — real trust signal, not a star spam.
- **Zero 3D illustration, zero gradient icons, zero emoji decoration.** Noto Emoji shows up only in page titles (🎄🟣🔴⭐️) as an SEO/tab-icon move, never on-page.

### Photography treatment
- **Hero:** Real photographs, often drone-aerial of a completed home/job. Dark-gradient overlay at 30–70% opacity so headline type is readable. **Not stock.** Not dotted with stock "smiling technician" energy.
- **Team photos:** Cut-out / clean-backdrop portraits (Anomaly Pool team grid). Occasional stock replacement where the client couldn't supply.
- **Service photos:** Real jobs, same client. Photo quality is noticeably higher on BtA builds than industry-average (drone + golden-hour photography pass is part of the deliverable).
- **No duotone, no grayscale, no cream-filter editorial treatment** — **full-color realistic photography** is the rule. Closest anyone gets to a photo effect is the dark-gradient hero overlay.
- **Kingswood is the outlier** — uses the same natural light rule but leans sun-dappled-estate editorial, closer to a luxury magazine spread.

## When to invoke "Be-The-Anomaly style" for a ProofPilot demo

**Use when the client is:**
- **Pool service, pool construction, pool remodel, tile cleaning** (BtA's flagship vertical — 7+ builds in the portfolio).
- **Landscape, outdoor living, outdoor lighting, tree service, hardscape, turf, holiday lighting** — everything around the home's outdoor perimeter.
- **Concrete, curbing, foundation, driveway contractor.**
- **Roofing, construction, remodeling, exterior services.**
- **Locksmith, auto detailing, auto services** (BtA has a DFW auto/trades niche).
- **Solar installation, solar-panel cleaning.**
- Any DFW/Denver/TX-adjacent trades brand where the buyer is a **middle-class to affluent homeowner** expecting a modern, clean-sans aesthetic (NOT a $500 shop-sign brand and NOT a $50k luxury-estate brand — sit in the middle).
- The client has supplied (or can be persuaded to produce) **real drone/job-site photography** — BtA's whole aesthetic relies on high-quality real photos.
- The client wants **warmer, longer-reading hero headlines** (title case, 70–80px) rather than shouting all-caps billboards.

**Don't use when:**
- The client is a **$50k+ luxury design-build firm, boutique hotel, premium real-estate developer, or high-end wedding venue** — go to the Kingswood luxury-serif variant instead or a different template entirely.
- The client name is generic "ABC Services" with no obvious word-level highlight noun — BtA's hero relies on a word to paint ("TRUSTED", "DFW POOL CLEANING", "LEGENDARY").
- The brand has **no budget for original photography** — BtA's aesthetic breaks when forced to use stock; 180 Sites tolerates stock better because of the strong illustration/motif work.
- The client is a **dentist, lawyer, financial advisor, therapist, pharmacy, or professional-services brand** — BtA's blue-collar-pride positioning won't transfer.
- The page needs to **shout from 30 feet** (a $99-sign-spin brand) — 180 Sites' Montserrat 900 / Saira 900 / Teko 900 uppercase route is louder and better-fit.

### Contrast with 180 Sites (where the two agencies diverge)
| Dimension | 180 Sites | Be The Anomaly |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Hero case | 70%+ ALL CAPS | ~50/50 title case / caps |
| Hero word highlight | Outline-stroked single word | Color-painted or block-highlighted single word |
| Display face | Montserrat 900 / Saira 900 / Teko 900 / Orbitron 900 | Bebas Neue 400 / Montserrat 800 / Russo One / Titillium Web |
| Body | Roboto / Barlow / Open Sans | Work Sans / Nunito Sans / Plus Jakarta Sans / Raleway |

| Accent saturation | Candy — `#FF9143`, `#F10800`, `#FFCD00` | Jewel — `#4CA8DE`, `#A91D3A`, `#0A660E` |

**The tl;dr:** BtA is to 180 Sites what **jewel tones are to candy colors, title case is to ALL CAPS, and a real photograph is to a mascot illustration.** Both reject serifs and cream-and-sand editorial aesthetics. BtA is *calmer, more design-forward, DFW-luxe* — 180 Sites is *louder, more mascot-driven, trade-show-billboard*.

## Concrete copy-forward moves

When Designer Brain is told "make it Be-The-Anomaly style" for a home-service demo, these moves are **required**:

1. **Pick one condensed/geometric sans for display.** Default to **Bebas Neue 400** at 70–80px title-case for pool/cleaning/pool-adjacent clients. For locksmith/auto/construction, substitute **Montserrat 800 uppercase**. For outdoor living/high-end trades, substitute **Russo One 400 title-case**. For tree/landscape (craft-y), substitute **Titillium Web 700 with a 6px letter-spacing outline-stroke**. Never Fraunces, Playfair, Merriweather, or any serif unless the explicit brief is $50k+ luxury design-build (in which case invoke the Kingswood route).

2. **Paint one noun in the H1 the accent color.** BtA's most portable move: keep the headline black/white, but color-highlight *one* noun via (a) solid color fill, (b) solid rectangular block behind the word, or (c) squiggle underline SVG under the word. Example: "WE MAKE **[DFW POOL CLEANING]** STRESS-FREE" or "DENVER METRO'S **[TRUSTED]** OUTDOOR LIVING EXPERTS" where the bracketed word is in accent color / block-highlighted.

3. **Pick one jewel-tone accent.** `#4CA8DE` cyan-blue (pool/water), `#A91D3A` crimson-red (outdoor living/roofing), `#0A660E` forest-green (landscape/tree), `#3AB636` kelly-green (locksmith/service-speed), `#0085AD` teal (concrete/construction), `#7A54CB` purple (agency/tech-forward). Pair with **tinted near-black (`#121A1E`, `#16181D`, `#061939`) and warm white/off-white (`#F6F6F6`, `#FFFCF4`).** Never `#000`, never pure cream, never beige.

4. **Primary button = accent bg, white text, 6–8px radius, Work Sans/Nunito 700, with a chunky offset shadow.** Use `box-shadow: 0 5px 0 0 [tinted near-black]` for the BtA "truck bumper sticker" button tell. Inverse pair = white bg, accent text. Labels: "Get a Free Quote" (default), "Request A Free Estimate" (outdoor living), "Book Now" (fast-service verticals like locksmith), "Free Estimate" (everything else). Never "Learn More" as the primary action.

5. **Alternate dark-accent sections with white/off-white sections.** Every other section flips bg color. Target 10–11 sections total: hero dark → stats dark-accent band → services dark → about white → benefits dark → guarantee accent-color band → reviews white → process dark → inline form accent-color band → FAQ white → footer dark.

6. **Lead with review proof.** Put a **Google 5-star pill with the real Google "G" logo** *inside the hero* above or below the H1 — not as a late-page section. "5.0 Google Reviews" with real star icons. BtA does this on 5 of 6 deep-dive builds; 180 Sites does not.

7. **One hero photograph, not a composite.** Use a single high-quality drone-aerial, twilight-exterior, or job-site photograph with a dark-gradient overlay (`linear-gradient(rgba(0,0,0,.35), rgba(0,0,0,.65))`). If the client can't supply real photography, lean into the Shaq move — find a local celebrity/authority figure to photograph with the owner. Do NOT default to stock "technician in front of white van."

8. **Inline embedded contact form in an accent-color section, near the bottom.** Not a modal. Not behind a CTA button. An actual `<form>` baked into the page body, on an accent-color bg, with a bold headline "Let's Build Something Great Together" / "Ready to Get Started?" and 4–6 fields plus primary CTA. This is BtA's canonical conversion block.

### Weaknesses and things NOT to copy
- **The exit-intent popup with a live 60-second timer** (Anomaly Pool) reads as aggressive/gimmicky — skip unless the client explicitly asks for urgency tactics.
- **Hand-drawn squiggle arrow + italic "People Can't Stop Raving!"** is cute once, tiresome on every build. Use sparingly.
- **The Shaq endorsement** is a one-off — don't fabricate celebrity endorsements the client doesn't actually have. Fall back to real customer portrait photography instead.
- **Kingswood's italic-serif route is correctly an outlier** — do not apply it to a standard pool/concrete/locksmith brief. It is reserved for $50k+ luxury design-build clients only.
- **Many BtA portfolio tiles have no live URL** (stale or unlaunched) — when sourcing inspiration, verify the live site exists before treating a portfolio tile as canon.
- **The "Other Industries" tab is weaker than the Blue Collar tab** — BtA's strongest work is squarely in trades. Don't reference their gym/wellness/restaurant builds as inspiration for a trades project.
