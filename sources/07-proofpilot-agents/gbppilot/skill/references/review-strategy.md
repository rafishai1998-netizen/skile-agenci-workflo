# Review Strategy

## Why reviews matter

Reviews are one of the clearest prominence signals in local search. Volume, velocity, recency, and response quality all influence how strong the profile looks to both Google and searchers.

A business with steady recent reviews often outperforms a business with a larger but stale review history.

## Velocity targets

Set the monthly target based on customer volume.

| Business size | Monthly review target |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Under 50 customers per month | 2 to 4 reviews |
| 50 to 200 customers per month | 5 to 10 reviews |
| Over 200 customers per month | 10 to 20 reviews |

## Request timing

Best timing:
- within 24 hours of successful service completion
- text first, then email follow-up
- personal follow-up if no review after two automated touches

## SMS template

Hi [FirstName], thanks again for choosing [BusinessName]. If we earned it, a quick Google review would help a lot. It only takes about 30 seconds: [review link]

## Email template

Subject: Quick favor, 30 seconds

Hi [FirstName],

Thanks for trusting [BusinessName] with your [service type]. If we did a good job, a Google review is the best thank-you we could get and helps more local customers find us.

[Review link]

Appreciate you,
[Owner or technician name]

## Response protocol

Every review should get a response within 48 hours.

### 5-star reviews
- thank them by name
- mention the service or detail they referenced
- keep it short and human

### 3 to 4-star reviews
- acknowledge the issue
- move the conversation offline quickly
- follow up the same day
- if resolved, politely ask whether they would consider updating the review

### 1 to 2-star reviews
- stay calm and professional
- do not argue publicly
- move offline fast
- escalate to owner or manager
- report only clearly fraudulent or policy-violating reviews

## Review cadence

Suggested system:
- Day 0: service complete
- Day 1: automated SMS
- Day 4: email follow-up
- Day 14: manual personal outreach if still no review

## Monthly metrics

Track:
- new reviews this month
- average star rating
- response rate
- average response time
- rolling 90-day review count

## Do not

- offer incentives for reviews
- ignore negative reviews
- batch-request once a year and call it done
- gate reviews by routing unhappy customers away from Google
