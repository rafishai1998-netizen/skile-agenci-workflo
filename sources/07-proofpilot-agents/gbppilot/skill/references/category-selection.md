# Primary and Secondary Category Selection

## The rule

The primary GBP category is the single strongest ranking signal you control. Pick the category that matches the search intent driving the highest-revenue customer. The primary category must also match the H1 and meta title theme of the client homepage. Mismatched primary categories are one of the fastest ways to weaken map-pack relevance.

## Primary category selection framework

Rank candidate categories against four criteria:

1. Revenue match: Does this category match the service that drives 60% or more of revenue?
2. Search demand: How many people in the service area search for this category per month?
3. Competition: Are the top 3 map-pack spots already dominated by entrenched competitors?
4. Homepage match: Does the website homepage clearly sell this service as the primary offer?

Score each candidate 1 to 5 on each criterion. Pick the highest total. Break ties by choosing the category with the clearest recurring-revenue match.

## Secondary category rules

Target 5 to 9 secondary categories. Google allows up to 10 total categories including the primary.

- Every secondary category should map to a real service offering, not a speculative one.
- Every secondary category should map to a dedicated website page or a clearly planned CREATE page.
- Rank each as HIGH, MEDIUM, or LOW priority based on expected impression share and fit.
- Do not pick secondary categories that contradict the primary.

## Common vertical starting points

### Property Management
- Primary candidates: Property Management Company, Real Estate Rental Agency, Apartment Rental Agency
- Default primary: Property Management Company
- Strong secondaries: Real Estate Rental Agency, Real Estate Agency, Apartment Rental Agency, Leasing Service, Property Maintenance, Condominium Rental Agency, Rental Agency, Real Estate Consultant

### Electrician
- Primary candidates: Electrician, Electrical Installation Service, Electrical Repair Shop
- Default primary: Electrician
- Strong secondaries: Electrical Installation Service, Electrical Repair Shop, Lighting Contractor, EV Charging Station Contractor, Solar Energy Contractor, Generator Shop

### Plumber
- Primary candidates: Plumber, Drainage Service, Water Heater Installation Service
- Default primary: Plumber
- Strong secondaries: Drainage Service, Water Heater Installation Service, Water Softening Equipment Supplier, Gas Installation Service, Emergency Plumber

### HVAC
- Primary candidates: HVAC Contractor, Air Conditioning Contractor, Heating Contractor
- Default primary: HVAC Contractor
- Strong secondaries: Air Conditioning Contractor, Heating Contractor, Furnace Repair Service, Air Duct Cleaning Service, Heat Pump Supplier

### Roofing
- Primary candidates: Roofing Contractor
- Default primary: Roofing Contractor
- Strong secondaries: Siding Contractor, Gutter Cleaning Service, Flat Roofing Contractor, Shingle Supplier

### Landscaping
- Primary candidates: Landscaper, Lawn Care Service
- Default primary: Landscaper
- Strong secondaries: Lawn Care Service, Tree Service, Landscape Designer, Irrigation Equipment Supplier, Garden Landscape Service

### Law Firm
- Primary candidates: narrow specialty category first, such as Personal Injury Attorney or Family Law Attorney
- Default primary: the narrow specialty, not the generic Law Firm label

### Dentist
- Primary candidates: Dentist, Cosmetic Dentist, Pediatric Dentist
- Default primary: Dentist unless the practice is specialty-only
- Strong secondaries: Cosmetic Dentist, Dental Implants Provider, Orthodontist, Pediatric Dentist, Emergency Dental Service

## Tiebreaker rules

When two categories look equally strong:
1. Pick the one that matches the homepage H1 verbatim or near-verbatim.
2. Pick the one with the higher recurring-revenue potential.
3. Pick the one with fewer locked-in dominant competitors.
4. Pick the broader category for top-of-funnel visibility, the narrower one for higher-intent conversions.

## Do not pick

- categories that do not describe what the business actually does today
- categories aimed at future services six months out
- obscure long-tail categories with weak match quality
- duplicate categories under slightly different names
