# Services and Products

## Services vs products

Google Business Profile exposes two separate fields.

### Services
Services are the full catalog of what the business offers. Think of Services as the website sitemap you want Google to trust. Every important service in the GBP should have a matching page on the website. Services reinforce long-tail relevance.

Target:
- 12 to 18 services for most local service businesses
- more is acceptable only when the list is clean, strategically grouped, and mirrored by the website

### Products
Products are the photo-forward, high-converting hero offers. Products surface visually in the knowledge panel and mobile profile views. They are especially strong when the business can show real work, guarantees, or public pricing.

Target:
- 8 to 12 products

A service can also be a product. The best offerings often belong in both places.

## Audit-first rule

Before recommending a new structure, inspect the public profile surface.

Capture four things separately:
- **Visible services**: what a consumer can actually see on the profile
- **Recommended backend services**: what should exist even if the full backend is not visible
- **Visible products**: what a consumer can actually see as product cards
- **Recommended products**: what should exist to improve both backend relevance and frontend conversion

Public visibility does not always equal backend completeness. Label that distinction clearly.

## How to handle a visible services list

When Google shows a public services list:
1. capture the exact labels first
2. note duplicate labels, capitalization issues, and obvious overlap
3. group the labels into clean canonical service clusters
4. map those canonical clusters to real website URLs or CREATE URLs
5. explain the strategy in a table, not only in prose

Example of the right thinking:
- public list might show `Water softener installation`, `Water softeners`, `Residential water softening`, and `Hard water solutions`
- the website should usually not build four separate near-duplicate pages just because Google exposed four labels
- instead, choose a canonical page like `Water Softener Installation` and then decide whether supporting subpages are needed

## Service mirroring matrix

Every serious GBP audit should include a one-to-one mirroring matrix.

Recommended columns:
- GBP main category or service cluster
- Visible GBP service label
- Recommended canonical website page
- Live URL or CREATE URL
- Status: EXISTS or CREATE
- Notes on consolidation, duplicates, or page-scope decisions

This matrix is where the strategy becomes implementation-ready.

## Services format

Each recommended service entry should include:
- Service name
- 1 to 2 sentence description
- URL
- Status badge: CREATE or EXISTS

### Service name rules
- match how customers search, not internal jargon
- include the city only when it reinforces real local intent
- keep it concise
- favor canonical service names over messy public duplicates

### Description rules
- lead with what it is
- include the differentiator second
- include pricing if public
- do not stuff keywords

### URL rules
- use the real site path if it exists
- if the page does not exist yet, provide the planned path and mark it CREATE
- do not pretend a URL is live if it will 404

## Products format

Each product entry should include:
- Product name
- Price or price label
- 2 to 3 sentence description
- Image brief

### Product audit rules
- say whether products were visible in the public profile
- if no products are visible, call that out as a miss unless there is a strong reason not to use them
- treat products as both a frontend conversion layer and a backend relevance layer
- for service businesses, use products to highlight flagship offers visually, not only literal retail items

### Image brief format

Use this shape:

- Subject: what the image shows
- Composition: framing, angle, and what appears in frame
- Overlay: exact headline or accent text if needed
- Specs: 1200x900 px, JPG, under 5 MB

### Product rules
- prioritize visual, high-converting offers
- use real work or realistic branded graphics where possible
- prefer true differentiators such as guarantees, speed, financing, emergency service, public pricing, or flagship services
- if the website has no strong service proof yet, build the product set around the clearest hero offers first

## Service-to-product mapping

Mark the strongest 8 to 12 services that should also exist as products.

Good product candidates usually have at least one of these:
- visual proof
- high revenue contribution
- clear public price or starting price
- strong differentiator
- strong buyer appeal in a card format

## Do not

- list services the business cannot actually deliver
- turn every messy GBP label into its own new page without consolidation logic
- create products with no real image concept
- rely on unrelated stock photography without a clear brief
- promise fake pricing or discounts
