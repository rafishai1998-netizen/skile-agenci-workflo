# Final QA Checklist for GBPPilot Deliverables

Run this before sharing the final document.

## Strategy logic

- Primary category clearly matches the highest-value service intent
- Homepage intent supports the chosen primary category
- Secondary categories represent real services, not speculative offers
- The category set is not bloated or contradictory

## Current surface audit

- The document clearly states what was publicly visible for posts, products, services, and areas served
- It labels unverified backend details as unverified instead of presenting them as facts
- It states whether visible posts were found in the last 30 to 60 days, or clearly says that no visible posts were found in the inspected public view
- It states whether products were visible, missing, underbuilt, or unverified
- It states whether a public services list was visible and whether it looked thin, strong, duplicated, or messy
- It states whether areas served were visible and whether obvious gaps were present

## Website mirroring

- Every important GBP service has a live URL or is explicitly tagged CREATE
- The mirroring matrix is present and easy to scan
- Messy public service labels are consolidated into clean canonical website architecture
- Mirroring tasks are actionable and testable
- NAP instructions are exact, not vague
- Schema, internal linking, and location support are included where relevant

## GBP system completeness

- Short and long business descriptions are present
- Services menu is complete and easy to scan
- Products include image briefs, not just product names
- Attributes are specific to the business
- Q and A seeds answer real buyer questions
- Posts calendar includes topic, cadence, and CTA destinations
- Review strategy includes both request cadence and response protocol

## Geo relevance and citations

- Geo relevance claims are tied to Local Falcon, Maps evidence, or another explicit source
- Heat map or grid observations are not overstated when no geo-grid evidence exists
- Citation or NAP findings are tied to real evidence when they are included

## Client-facing writing quality

- Clear, plain language
- No em dashes
- No semicolons
- No generic filler like "comprehensive" unless truly justified
- Recommendations explain why they matter

## ProofPilot presentation

- Document sections follow the promised order
- Tables are readable and consistent
- Summary is easy to scan
- Next steps are obvious

## Evidence hygiene

- Claims are tied back to live site, visible profile, competitor, citation, or data-pull evidence
- Any missing evidence is labeled clearly
- Unverified profile details are not presented as facts
