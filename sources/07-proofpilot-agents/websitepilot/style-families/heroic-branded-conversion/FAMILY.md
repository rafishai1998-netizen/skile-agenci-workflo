# Heroic Branded Conversion

## Use this when

- the brand has a strong name, emblem, animal, shield, bolt, badge, or founder-led identity
- the site needs energy and memorability, not just polite trust
- the CTA needs to hit early and repeat hard
- the company sells urgent or confidence-heavy local service

## Do not use this when

- the client is selling luxury restraint
- the proof is all project photography and taste
- the service model is consultation-heavy and slow

## Visual thesis

This family should feel like the brand has a point of view.
Not just competent.
Recognizable.
Confident.
Fast.

## Structural habits

- giant statement hero
- badge or review capsule near the hero
- service cards with hard edges and visible attitude
- repeated CTA bands
- process broken into bold sequential steps
- testimonials as proof anchors, not soft filler

## Typography rules

- uppercase display headlines are normal here
- the headline should feel emblematic, not editorial
- support copy stays tighter than the other families

## Color rules

- one dark anchor
- one high-energy signal color
- one supporting accent if the brand can carry it
- never let the palette drift into generic contractor red/blue defaults without brand permission

## Motion rules

- stronger hover states are acceptable
- reveals can be snappier
- buttons should feel tactile

## Remove-the-logo test

Without the logo, the page should still read as:

- a specific trade
- a company with personality
- a high-conviction local operator
- a site built to convert now, not browse later
