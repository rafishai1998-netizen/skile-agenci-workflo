# Clean Recurring Service

## Use this when

- the business sells recurring care
- reliability and friendliness matter more than drama
- the proof is badges, reviews, technicians, and repeat service photos
- the offer is quote, inspection, or simple recurring plan

## Do not use this when

- the client needs luxury editorial restraint
- the identity depends on a heavy mascot or themed world
- the service is pure high-ticket showcase work

## Visual thesis

This family should feel bright, responsive, and organized.
The customer should immediately feel that maintenance will be easier after one call.

## Structural habits

- top trust badges
- clean hero with specific offer
- repeating image rails
- service scope cards with what's included
- inspection or quote form nearby
- review strip and certifications

## Color rules

- use brighter palettes only when the brand can carry them
- support the clean feeling with white space and soft panels
- avoid neon tech palettes unless the brand already has them

## Remove-the-logo test

Without the logo, the page should still say:

- recurring service
- trustworthy technicians
- easy quote path
- low-friction maintenance brand
