# Premium Outdoor Editorial

## Use this when

- the client sells high-ticket outdoor projects
- the real proof is project photography and taste
- the customer is buying design judgment, not just labor
- the brand needs to feel restrained, expensive, and composed

## Do not use this when

- the service is urgent, cheap, or highly reactive
- the company needs loud, repeated CTA pressure
- there are no project visuals to support an editorial layout

## Visual thesis

This family should feel like an outdoor design studio that also knows how to close.
The page must feel curated before it feels promotional.

## Structural habits

- photography-led hero
- editorial section rhythm with big negative space
- project grid or case-study strip
- metrics that read like qualifications, not coupon stats
- softer but still clear CTA points

## Typography rules

- a refined display face with tension
- roomy subheads
- fewer all-caps moments

## Color rules

- dark neutrals, stone, sand, forest, bronze, or similar brand-led tones
- avoid candy colors
- contrast should come from composition, not shouting

## Remove-the-logo test

Without the logo, the page should still say:

- design-build
- premium work
- project-led trust
- intentional taste
