# StrategyPilot SEO Director Playbook

Use this reference when you need deeper strategy thinking than a normal audit.

## Core Job

A real SEO director does not just say what is wrong.
A real SEO director decides:
- what pages should exist
- what pages should not exist
- how the website should be structured
- what questions the market is asking
- what competitors built that matters
- what strategic content could help the brand win in search and AI answers
- what should be built first, second, and later

## Non-Negotiable Thinking Rules

1. Start with business reality.
   Do not mistake template junk, lazy agency copy, or leftover placeholder content for real company strategy.

2. Judge page quality like a customer.
   Ask:
   - Is this actually useful?
   - Is this specific?
   - Is this easy to scan?
   - Does this answer the real question?
   - Does this feel trustworthy?
   - Does this feel like it was written with care?

3. Think in page systems, not random pages.
   One page rarely wins by itself. Winning sites usually have a connected system.

4. Tie content to real demand.
   If people are asking it on Google, Reddit, People Also Ask, forums, sales calls, reviews, and support tickets, it belongs in the strategy.

5. Make the advice easy to follow.
   A client should be able to skim the strategy and instantly see what to build next.

## Content Quality Scorecard

Use this on key pages.

### Strong page
- clear headline
- specific promise
- obvious local or niche relevance when needed
- helpful subheadings
- answers real buyer questions
- includes proof, examples, reviews, process, or expertise
- clear CTA
- feels original and useful

### Weak page
- vague headline
- generic intro
- repetitive or fluffy copy
- thin sections
- no real proof
- weak structure
- no strong FAQ or decision support
- feels like AI or template filler

When useful, label pages as:
- strong
- decent but needs work
- thin and generic
- unfinished
- template residue

### Sources to check

### Questions to look for

- safety questions
- maintenance questions
- mistakes to avoid
- best provider questions
- local recommendation questions

## Strategic Content Opportunities Most People Miss

These often create outsized value when done well and honestly.

### 1. Best-of pages
Examples:
- best pest control company in Scottsdale
- best HVAC company in Mesa
- best med spa in Gilbert
- best payroll software for small businesses

Why they matter:
- strong comparison intent
- can earn AI citations
- help the brand define the market instead of waiting for others to do it

Execution rule:
- these pages should be genuinely useful, not obvious spam
- it is often smart to include the client as a top option while also naming real competitors
- if written clearly and credibly, they can become one of the few pages in a market that AI systems repeatedly reference

### 2. Cost pages
Examples:
- pest control cost in Phoenix
- how much does X service cost
- what affects the cost of Y

Why they matter:
- buyers ask cost questions before converting
- strong lower-funnel intent
- useful for both SEO and sales enablement

### 3. Who is this for pages
Examples:
- pest control for restaurants
- bookkeeping for law firms
- payroll for construction companies
- orthodontics for teens vs adults

Why they matter:
- sharpens audience relevance
- supports segment and commercial SEO

### 4. Problem pages
Examples:
- why do scorpions keep coming back
- signs you have termites
- why your AC smells musty
- why your payroll is always late

Why they matter:
- captures people before they search for the exact service name
- great bridge from informational intent into commercial intent

### 5. Decision pages
Examples:
- one-time pest spray vs recurring service
- DIY vs professional termite treatment
- monthly bookkeeping vs in-house accountant

Why they matter:
- catches buyers at the exact decision stage
- builds trust by helping them choose

## Architecture Patterns by Business Type

## Local service business
Typical structure:
- homepage
- residential hub
- commercial hub
- core service pages
- city pages
- service plus city pages
- pest or problem hub if relevant
- proof pages
- FAQ or resources hub

If the business has many service variations under one category, consider an extra topical hub.
Example for pest control:
- residential hub
- commercial hub
- all pests hub
- individual pest pages under that hub
This gives the site a cleaner hierarchy and stronger topical relevance.

## Multi-location local service business
Typical structure:
- homepage
- location hub
- each location page
- service pages
- service plus city or service plus location matrix
- local proof on each location page

## SaaS or software
Typical structure:
- homepage
- solutions hub
- feature pages
- use-case pages
- industry pages
- comparison pages
- integrations pages
- pricing pages
- help or learning hub

## Ecommerce
Typical structure:
- homepage
- collection pages
- product pages
- brand pages
- use-case pages
- comparison pages
- buying guides
- FAQ and support pages

## Agency or consulting
Typical structure:
- homepage
- service hub
- service pages
- industry pages
- case studies
- methodology or process page
- comparison and authority pages
- insights hub

## Blog and Content Pillar Strategy

Do not treat the blog like a dumping ground.

Use a hub-and-spoke model:
- one pillar page or hub page for the big topic
- multiple supporting articles around subtopics
- every spoke links back to the hub
- the hub links out to the spokes and to the main money pages

HubSpot and Semrush both frame this as pillar pages, topic clusters, and subtopic support. Use that model when the site needs topical authority.

### Good content pillar examples for a local service brand
- scorpion control hub
- rodent control hub
- commercial pest control hub
- termite prevention hub
- mosquito control hub

### Good spoke article examples
- what attracts scorpions to your home
- when scorpions are most active in Arizona
- how often pest control should be done
- signs you need rodent exclusion
- what a commercial pest inspection includes
- pest control cost in Scottsdale

## Service Plus City Matrix

Use this when local intent is strong.

Examples:
- scorpion control Scottsdale
- rodent control Phoenix
- mosquito control Mesa

Rules:
- only build pages where there is real demand or business value
- do not mass-produce thin duplicates
- each page needs unique local relevance, proof, and internal links
- strongest cities and strongest services usually come first

## Google Business Profile Connection Layer

For local SEO strategies, include the website to GBP connection.

Check and recommend:
- primary category
- secondary categories
- services list
- product pages where relevant
- posts
- review flow
- photos
- landing page selection
- service page links from GBP where possible
- location consistency between website and GBP

The goal is to make the website and GBP reinforce each other.

## What Competitor Analysis Should Produce

Do not stop at naming competitors.
Produce a plain-language output like:
- competitors have these pages and you do not
- competitors go deeper on these services
- competitors have these city pages and segment pages
- competitors answer these buyer questions better
- competitors have stronger proof or structure on these pages

## Recommended Deliverable Structure

1. Executive summary
2. What is good right now
3. What is weak right now
4. Strategic focus
5. Pages competitors have that you do not
6. Service pages to build or improve
7. Location pages to build or improve
8. Service plus city matrix to build next
9. Segment or industry pages to build
10. Problem and FAQ content to build
11. Blog content pillars and spoke topics
12. Recommended website structure
13. Priority roadmap
14. Optional GBP recommendations for local businesses

## Presentation Pattern

When the strategy needs to feel more actionable, present the page plan in grouped tables:
- core site structure
- service page map
- location page map
- service plus city matrix
- content pillar and spoke map
- internal linking map

This makes the strategy easier to follow without turning it into a proposal.

## Final Test

Before finalizing a strategy, ask:
- Would an SEO director actually say this?
- Is it easy for a client to understand?
- Does it clearly show what to build next?
- Does it separate real strategy from junk template noise?
- Does it include both obvious foundations and smart opportunities most competitors miss?
