# ROI and Funnel Math for StrategyPilot

Use this reference any time you are building a strategy or audit that should tie SEO work to business impact.

## Why This Matters

Business owners do not just want page ideas.
They want to know:
- what those pages could rank for
- how much traffic that could create
- how many leads that traffic could become
- what those leads could be worth
- what the upside looks like if SEO is working well

## Core Rule

Always include a simple three-case model at the end of the strategy or audit:
- conservative
- realistic
- aggressive

This is not a fake promise.
It is a directional business case.
Use clear assumptions and say they are estimates.

Both strategy and audit versions should also include:
- lead economics
- cost of waiting
- total ROI framing

## Inputs to Gather

Use as many of these as you can:
- keyword volume for core service terms
- keyword volume for city + service terms
- competitor keywords and ranking patterns
- CPC values as a value proxy
- current organic visibility
- review strength
- likely CTR based on ranking position
- estimated site conversion rate
- average ticket value or average customer value
- close rate if known

If you do not know all of them, use reasonable ranges.

## Math Framework

### Step 1: Estimate reachable search demand
Build a realistic pool of the highest-value keywords the business should be able to rank for.

Split them into buckets:
- core money keywords
- service plus city keywords
- question and comparison keywords

Then estimate total monthly search demand.

### Step 2: Estimate clicks
Use ranking-based CTR assumptions.

Safe default CTR ranges:
- position 1-3: 18% to 35%
- position 4-5: 8% to 15%
- position 6-10: 2% to 8%

Model three cases:
- conservative
- realistic
- aggressive

### Step 3: Estimate leads
Traffic x conversion rate = estimated leads

Safe default conversion ranges for local service SEO:
- low: 2%
- mid: 4%
- strong: 6%+

Adjust when the site has strong reviews, trust, and clear CTAs.

### Step 4: Estimate customers or jobs sold
Leads x close rate = booked jobs or customers

If close rate is unknown, say so and either:
- stop at lead value
- or use a simple range like 30% to 60% depending on the business

### Step 5: Estimate revenue
Booked jobs x average ticket = estimated monthly revenue

If average ticket is unknown, use a conservative estimated range and label it.

### Step 6: Estimate total ROI
Estimate total ROI using the expected revenue upside against the expected investment.

When helpful, include:
- monthly ROI
- annualized ROI
- customer lifetime value upside if the business has recurring revenue or repeat business

If lifetime value is relevant, say so clearly.
For example, recurring service businesses should not be modeled only on the first ticket.

## Good Output Pattern

### Example output pattern
If AP ranked well for its top service and city keywords, the model could show:

- Conservative case
  - qualified traffic per month
  - estimated leads
  - estimated booked jobs
  - estimated revenue
  - estimated ROI

- Realistic case
  - qualified traffic per month
  - estimated leads
  - estimated booked jobs
  - estimated revenue
  - estimated ROI

- Aggressive case
  - qualified traffic per month
  - estimated leads
  - estimated booked jobs
  - estimated revenue
  - estimated ROI

That is the kind of model a business owner understands.

## Important Rules

1. Use ranges, not fake precision.
2. Separate conservative, likely, and dream-state thinking when helpful.
3. Explain the assumptions in plain English.
4. Use competitor visibility and CPC to support the upside.
5. Call out that keyword volume is directional, not perfect.
6. When volume is weak but intent is strong, say so.
7. If the business has no average ticket value, mark your ticket assumptions clearly.

## Best Places to Use This in the Deliverable

### In a strategy doc
Use near the end as:
- Revenue and ROI Model
- Conservative, Realistic, and Aggressive Funnel Model
- What Better SEO Could Be Worth
- Cost of Waiting
- Lead Economics

### In an audit
Use as:
- Revenue Impact
- Conservative, Realistic, and Aggressive Funnel Model
- Cost of Waiting
- Lead Economics
- Search Value Being Left on the Table

## Plain-Language Framing

Use wording like:
- If this site ranked the way it should, this is what the funnel could look like.
- These are estimates, but they show the size of the opportunity.
- Even if actual results land below the dream case, the upside is still large.
- This is why the page strategy matters. It affects leads and revenue, not just traffic.

Avoid wording like:
- guaranteed ROI
- exact traffic forecast
- exact lead forecast
- exact revenue prediction

## Final Check

Before finalizing, ask:
- Did I connect the SEO plan to business impact?
- Did I explain the assumptions clearly?
- Did I use ranges instead of fake certainty?
- Would a business owner understand why this matters?
