# StrategyPilot Defaults

Use these as the standard default for all future StrategyPilot strategy documents.

## Core content rules

1. Include a **Search Atlas-style current rankings snapshot** near the front of the strategy.
2. The snapshot should emphasize:
   - branded vs non-branded keyword visibility
   - top ranking keywords with positions, estimated traffic, and search volume
   - whether current traffic is mostly branded
   - whether non-branded terms rank in top 3 / top 1
   - map pack / local pack visibility, especially whether only branded terms appear there
3. Use this section to explain whether SEO is actually generating demand or whether most visible traffic is just branded lookups.

## Layout and hierarchy rules

1. Use **hub** terminology, not **family**, when organizing service and location structures.
2. Present roadmaps and architecture in a **hierarchy-first layout**:
   - main hub page first
   - subpages clearly nested underneath
   - city pages grouped underneath the parent service or location hub
3. The structure should read like a blueprint, sitemap, or rollout plan, not a flat list.
4. When possible, make the hierarchy visually obvious with labels like Hub, arrows, indentation, grouped blocks, or parent-child organization.

## Table conventions

1. In recommended page-system or roadmap tables, **do not use a Priority column**.
2. Replace Priority with **estimated local search volume** or local keyword demand.
3. Search-volume figures can be directional estimates based on the initial research and do not need fake precision.
4. For service hubs and subpages, show the relevant local demand behind why each page should exist.

## Insurance + city page rule for applicable healthcare / therapy brands

When insurance acceptance materially affects how patients choose a provider, include a dedicated page family for:
- therapist or counselor + insurance + city
- psychologist + insurance + city
- examples like `Therapist in Fountain Valley Accepting Aetna` or `Counselor in Orange County Accepting Anthem Blue Cross`

Use this only when:
- the site clearly accepts the carrier
- the carrier matters to patient selection
- the practice can support the page with real providers, intake details, and insurance guidance

Default high-priority carriers when supported by the client:
- Aetna
- Anthem Blue Cross

Presentation rule:
- Treat these as a real strategic page type, not a throwaway FAQ.
- Group them under their own insurance-access or insurance-plus-location section in the architecture.
- Explain that they help capture local patients searching for in-network or insurance-accepted care.

## ROI modeling rule for StrategyPilot docs

When a strategy document includes ROI, do not stop at one simple traffic-to-revenue estimate.
Default to a two-path model when relevant:
- website only
- website plus SEO

For each path, show:
- total annual visitors or qualified organic visits
- visitor-to-lead conversion
- total leads
- lead-to-patient or lead-to-customer conversion
- total new patients or customers
- average monthly patient/customer value while active
- estimated lifetime value per patient/customer
- year-one realized revenue
- lifetime value created by year-one acquisitions
- annual investment cost
- net return, ROI multiple, and break-even timing

Presentation rule:
- Make the ramp obvious across 12 months.
- Explain that website work can create earlier conversion wins, while SEO compounds more heavily later in the year.
- When useful, add quarter-by-quarter ramp tables and a month-12 run-rate view.

## Writing intent

- Make the strategy obvious to a non-SEO client.
- Keep headings and callouts client-facing. Avoid internal sales phrasing like "best way to position the offer."
- Show what they are missing, what they should have, and how the page system fits together.
- Keep the story consistent across current snapshot, hub structure, site architecture, and service-plus-city rollout.

## Pitfalls to avoid

- Do not flatten hierarchy into generic matrices without clear parent-child structure.
- Do not use vague "priority" labels when search demand is the real justification.
- Do not present branded rankings as if they prove strong SEO performance.
