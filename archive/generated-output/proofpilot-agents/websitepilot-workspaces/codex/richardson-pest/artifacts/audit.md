# Lightweight WebsitePilot Audit

## Current strengths

- Strong, defensible origin story: Nelson Richardson started the company after his young daughter was stung by a scorpion.
- Clear specialization in scorpions and termites, which are high-anxiety Arizona pest concerns.
- Real local proof: father-son owned and operated, East Valley service area, Saturday hours, license number, real service photos.
- Specific service process: wall void flush, cinder-block wall void flush, vegetation base treatment, every-other-month maintenance.
- Strong CTA asset: same-day exterminating now available and visible phone number.

## Current weaknesses

- The existing design feels generic and low-contrast, so the brand's urgency and protection story are muted.
- The popup interrupts the first impression and covers the hero.
- High-value trust details appear too low on the page instead of supporting the first conversion decision.
- Service navigation is information-rich but visually cluttered.
- The logo palette is underused. The yellow scorpion accent should drive hierarchy and memory.

## Demo improvement mandate

Build a sharper first impression: dark protective hero, amber scorpion signal, real technician imagery, and a CTA that makes the next action obvious. Bring the family-safety story, same-day availability, and East Valley service area into the first scroll. Keep the page polished enough to feel custom, but grounded enough that a homeowner trusts it immediately.
